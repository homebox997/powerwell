Old Dog Stomach Problem Remedies

Blog • Published July 09, 2026 • 1 min read

Senior dogs experience stomach problems more frequently than younger dogs. Slower digestion, reduced stomach acid, dental issues, and underlying kidney or liver conditions can all contribute to vomiting, diarrhoea, gas, or loss of appetite in ageing dogs.

Common causes of stomach upset in older dogs include dietary changes, food intolerances, eating something inappropriate (scavenging), stress, side effects of medication, and chronic conditions such as pancreatitis, inflammatory bowel disease, or kidney disease. A sudden or severe change in appetite, vomiting bile, or diarrhoea lasting more than 24 hours should be evaluated by a veterinarian promptly.

Mild stomach upset can often be managed at home with simple remedies. A 12 to 24 hour fast (for healthy dogs, but not for puppies or dogs with certain medical conditions) gives the digestive system a chance to settle. After fasting, a bland diet of boiled chicken (skinless, boneless) and white rice in small, frequent meals is a standard veterinary recommendation. Gradually transition back to the regular food over 3 to 5 days once symptoms resolve.

Pumpkin puree (plain, not pie filling) can help both diarrhoea and constipation — one to two tablespoons mixed into a meal is a good starting point. Probiotics formulated for dogs can support healthy gut bacteria. Bone broth (low sodium, no onion or garlic) provides hydration and gentle nutrition for a dog that is reluctant to eat.

When to call the vet: repeated vomiting, blood in vomit or stool, signs of pain (whining, restlessness, hunched posture), distended belly, extreme lethargy, or failure to improve within 24 hours. Older dogs can dehydrate quickly, so prompt veterinary care is essential if symptoms are more than mild.
