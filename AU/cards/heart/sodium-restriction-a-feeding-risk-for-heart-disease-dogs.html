Sodium Restriction: A Feeding Risk for Heart Disease Dogs

heart 2026-07-18

While moderate sodium restriction is often recommended for dogs with heart disease, severe restriction can be risky, especially in early MMVD stages. Overly aggressive salt reduction may activate the renin-angiotensin-aldosterone system (RAAS), potentially worsening volume overload and cardiac remodeling. For dogs in stages B1 and B2, a balanced diet with moderate sodium levels is generally safer than a severely restricted diet. Always consult a veterinary nutritionist before making drastic dietary changes. Avoid high-salt treats like cheese, deli meats, and commercial dog biscuits with added salt.
