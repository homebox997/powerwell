Has anyone switched to fresh/freeze-dried food? Cost vs benefit?

👤 Kate P. 📅 2026-07-11 🐕 Zara, Labrador 💬 8 comments ❤️ 160 likes 👁 1610 views
Healthy 8-year-old Labrador on regular dry food. Considering fresh food diet. Is the 3x cost justified? Seeking honest experiences from people who've switched.
Zara's been on regular dry food her whole life. She's healthy, good weight, vet's always been happy. But I've been reading about fresh food diets and wondering if I'm missing something. The cost is significantly more - like 3x what I'm paying now. Is it actually worth it for an otherwise healthy dog? Or am I being marketed to? Interested in hearing from people who've made the switch - what changed (if anything)? Did your dog seem better? Worse? Same?

8 Comments

Karen H. 2026-07-11

Fresh food diets have come SUCH a long way. We use a local Melbourne service (Butcher's Dog Food) — pre-portioned, delivered frozen, our lab loves it. About $90/week for a lab size portion.

Tracy L. 2026-07-11

The cooked fresh food from services like 'The Honest Kitchen' is a good middle ground — not raw, balanced nutrition, smells amazing. Our golden demolished it.

Dave T. 2026-07-11

Be careful with DIY fresh food — getting the calcium:phosphorus ratio right is critical for senior dogs. Consult a vet nutritionist or use a formulated fresh food service.

Brett C. 2026-07-11

We spent 3 months on a raw diet then switched to gently cooked. Gently cooked seemed to be the sweet spot for our beagle's digestion and energy levels.

Rob F. 2026-07-11

The cost is real — $90-120/week for a large dog. But we saved $400 in vet dental bills last year because the fresh food naturally cleaned his teeth. Sometimes it balances out.

Nina B. 2026-07-11

Has anyone tried the subscription fresh food services like 'Doggie Delights' in NSW? Interested in hearing about variety and portion sizes for senior labs.

Gary M. 2026-07-11

We supplement kibble with fresh components even if we're not fully fresh feeding — a bit of lean mince, some veg, an egg. Bridges the gap without the full cost.

Sue P. 2026-07-11

Kelpies and working breeds need more protein than most senior formulas provide. If you're going fresh, make sure it's high protein. Our kelpie thrived on 70% meat + 30% veg mix.
