Veterinarian: CRP is a Screening Tool, Not a Diagnosis

heart 2026-07-19

Dr. Gavazza's team notes that while CRP is sensitive for inflammation, it is not specific to heart disease. It can be elevated in infections, trauma, or other conditions. In cardiac patients, a high CRP should prompt further investigation (echocardiography, X-rays, troponin) rather than be used alone. It is best for monitoring response to therapy or detecting complications early, not for initial diagnosis.
