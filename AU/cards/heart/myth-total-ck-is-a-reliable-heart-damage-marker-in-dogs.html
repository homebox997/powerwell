Myth: Total CK is a Reliable Heart Damage Marker in Dogs

heart 2026-07-19

Many believe total creatine kinase (CK) is a specific indicator of heart damage in dogs, but this is false. CK has three isoenzymes, and only CK-MB is found in the myocardium. Total CK increases with any muscle damage, including skeletal muscle injury from exercise or trauma. Additionally, its plasma half-life is under 3 hours, so it drops quickly. For accurate heart assessment, veterinarians rely on specific biomarkers like cardiac troponin or natriuretic peptides instead.
