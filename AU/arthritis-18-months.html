Milo's 18-Month Arthritis Journey: What Actually Worked

After being diagnosed with moderate osteoarthritis at age 10, Milo has been on a combination of glucosamine, MSM and fish oil for 18 months. Here is the complete breakdown of what changed, what did not, and what we wish we knew earlier.

The Diagnosis: What the Vet Found

Milo was 10 years old when our vet confirmed moderate osteoarthritis in his hips and both elbows. He had been slowing down on walks, struggling to get up from his bed, and seemed stiff first thing in the morning.

What surprised us most was how gradual it had been. We almost did not notice — we just thought he was getting older. But when the vet showed us the X-rays, it was clear: the cartilage in his hip joints had worn down significantly.

Understanding Severity Levels

Arthritis severity varies significantly. Here is how vets typically classify it:

🟢
Mild
Slight stiffness, occasional limp. Still active and happy.
Supplements + weight management
~$30–80/month
🟡
Moderate
Noticeable stiffness, reduced activity. Pain visible some days.
Supplements + vet pain medication
~$80–200/month
🔴
Severe
Significant pain, difficulty moving. Quality of life impacted.
Vet medication + surgery evaluation
$1,000+/month

Exercise & Lifestyle Changes

Supplements alone are not enough. Here is what we changed at home:

Morning Routine

We give Milo his supplements mixed into his breakfast — he does not even notice them. We keep walks to 20 minutes on soft grass in the mornings when he is least stiff.

Swimming Therapy

The single best thing we did was start swimming sessions. Milo loves our backyard pool — the vet confirmed that swimming is the best exercise for arthritic dogs because it builds muscle without loading the joints. We do 15–20 minutes, 2–3 times per week in summer.

Home Modifications

  • Memory foam orthopedic bed (~$180, Big Barker — expensive but genuinely the best)
  • Non-slip rugs on all hard floors to prevent slipping
  • Ramp for the car boot (~$30, DIY from Bunnings)
  • Raised food bowls to reduce neck strain

18-Month Results: What Worked & What Did Not

What worked: Consistency with supplements. We never skip a dose. Green-lipped mussel was the biggest game-changer for Milo's elbow pain. Swimming built up his back leg muscles noticeably.

What did not work: We tried a cheap glucosamine from eBay — it made zero difference. Brand matters. We also wasted money on a cooling gel bed (not appropriate for arthritis dogs — they need warmth, not cooling).

The biggest lesson: Start supplements early. The earlier you catch it, the better the results. Milo's elbows will never be perfect, but he is active, happy, and moves like a much younger dog.
