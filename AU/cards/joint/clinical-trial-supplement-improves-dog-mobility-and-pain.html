Clinical Trial: Supplement Improves Dog Mobility and Pain

joint 2026-07-16

In a 2025 double-blind, placebo-controlled study, 40 dogs with osteoarthritis were randomly assigned to receive either a test supplement (fish oil, ASU, turmeric, Boswellia, grape seed extract) or a placebo for 56 days. The test group showed statistically significant reductions in pain assessed by a validated Canine Brief Pain Inventory (CBPI), and improved mobility measured by owner and veterinarian questionnaires. For example, the pain interference score dropped by 40% in the supplement group versus 12% in placebo. This study provides evidence that a multi-ingredient nutraceutical can effectively manage OA symptoms in dogs.

Tags: clinical data, randomized controlled trial, pain score, mobility, supplement efficacy
Source: Frontiers | A randomized, double-blind, controlled study on the efficacy of an oral dietary supplement containing fish oil, ASU and phytotherapeutic extracts in canine osteoarthritis
