Veterinarian: MVR Benefits Outweigh Risks for PH Dogs

heart 2026-07-18

Dr. Tomohiko Yoshida and colleagues from Obihiro University and University of Florida emphasize that mitral valve repair (MVR) can be a valuable treatment option for dogs with pulmonary hypertension (PH) secondary to myxomatous mitral valve disease (MMVD). Their study demonstrated that most dogs experienced significant improvement in cardiac function and clinical signs after surgery. However, they caution that 9.5% of dogs had residual PH and 14.2% developed late PH recurrence, suggesting some dogs may have underlying pulmonary arterial pathology. Therefore, careful patient selection and long-term postoperative monitoring are essential for optimal outcomes.
