When a Dog's Gait Turns Asymmetric: Recognizing a Limp

joint 2026-07-16

A limp, or lameness, is an asymmetric abnormality in a dog's gait. It can manifest as an antalgic gait, where the dog puts less weight on the painful leg and holds it in the air for a shorter time. In severe cases, the dog may refuse to walk. A limp can also be caused by weakness or deformity, not just pain. For example, hip weakness may cause a Trendelenburg gait, where the dog's body shifts over the affected hip. Observing your dog's walking pattern is the first step in identifying joint or mobility issues.
