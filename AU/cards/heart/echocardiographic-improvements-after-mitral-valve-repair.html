Echocardiographic Improvements After Mitral Valve Repair

heart 2026-07-18

In a study of 21 dogs with pulmonary hypertension (PH) secondary to myxomatous mitral valve disease (MMVD), mitral valve repair led to significant improvements in echocardiographic measures. Before surgery, 12 dogs were ACVIM Stage C and 9 were Stage D. Postoperative measurements showed significant decreases in normalized left ventricular internal diameter in diastole (LVIDDN), mitral E wave velocity, left atrial to aortic ratio (LA/Ao), and tricuspid regurgitation velocity (all p < 0.01). These changes indicate reduced left heart pressure and improved cardiac function. Complete resolution of clinical signs was achieved in 71.4% of dogs after surgery.
