Collagen Shows Promise but Needs More Research for Pets

joint 2026-07-16

Collagen supplements, particularly undenatured Type II collagen, showed some promise for managing osteoarthritis in pets according to a 2022 review. However, most of the available trials were of lower quality, featuring small sample sizes, weak methods, and subjective assessments. While collagen could be helpful, the evidence is not yet strong enough to make it a top recommendation. Pet owners interested in trying collagen should view it as a potential complementary therapy and should discuss it with their veterinarian, while waiting for more high-quality research to confirm its benefits.
