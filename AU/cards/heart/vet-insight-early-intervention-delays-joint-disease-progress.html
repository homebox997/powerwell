Vet Insight: Early Intervention Delays Joint Disease Progression

heart 2026-07-18

Veterinary orthopedic specialists emphasize that early detection and intervention can significantly slow the progression of osteoarthritis in dogs. Dr. Julie Smith, a board-certified veterinary surgeon, states that 'once you see visible lameness, joint damage is already moderate to severe.' She recommends annual orthopedic exams for at-risk breeds starting at age 2, including palpation and range-of-motion assessment. Early intervention with joint supplements, weight control, and modified activity can delay the need for pain medication and surgery by years.
