Max the Labrador: From Limping to Leaping

joint 2026-07-16

Max, an 8-year-old Labrador Retriever, struggled with stiffness and limping after walks. His owner tried a new oral supplement containing fish oil, ASU, and herbal extracts as part of a clinical study. Within 4 weeks, Max's owner noticed he was more willing to go for longer walks and no longer hesitated to jump onto the sofa. By day 56, his vet reported improved joint flexibility and reduced pain scores. Max's case mirrors the study's findings—many dogs experienced better quality of life without needing strong painkillers. His owner now gives the supplement daily and continues with gentle exercise to keep Max active.

Tags: real case, Labrador, supplement success, mobility improvement, joint care
Source: Frontiers | A randomized, double-blind, controlled study on the efficacy of an oral dietary supplement containing fish oil, ASU and phytotherapeutic extracts in canine osteoarthritis
