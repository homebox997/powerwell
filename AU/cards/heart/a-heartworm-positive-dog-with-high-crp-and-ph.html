A Heartworm-Positive Dog with High CRP and PH

heart 2026-07-19

A 5-year-old mixed-breed dog from a shelter tested positive for heartworms. It had a mild cough and was lethargic. Blood work showed elevated CRP at 28 mg/L. Echocardiography revealed pulmonary hypertension (PH) with right ventricular enlargement. The high CRP suggested active arteritis from heartworm infection. After heartworm treatment and pulmonary vasodilators, CRP normalized to 8 mg/L, and the dog's energy improved. This case shows CRP's value in tracking inflammation.
