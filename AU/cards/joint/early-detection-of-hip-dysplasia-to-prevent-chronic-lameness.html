Early Detection of Hip Dysplasia to Prevent Chronic Lameness

joint 2026-07-16

Hip dysplasia is a common developmental condition that can cause a non-painful limp or later lead to arthritis and pain. Early detection through regular vet check-ups and screening X-rays (e.g., PennHIP or OFA evaluation) is crucial, especially in large-breed puppies. Preventive measures include maintaining a lean body weight, avoiding over-exercise on hard surfaces during growth, and feeding a balanced diet with controlled calcium and phosphorus. Early intervention can slow disease progression and improve quality of life, reducing the risk of chronic lameness later in life.
