Omega-3 Fatty Acids for Canine Heart Health Support

heart 2026-07-18

Omega-3 fatty acids (EPA and DHA) are commonly recommended as supportive supplements for dogs with heart disease, including MMVD. Typical dosage ranges from 40 to 100 mg/kg of combined EPA and DHA per day, given with food to improve absorption. These fatty acids have anti-inflammatory properties and may help reduce the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines and oxidative stress associated with cardiac remodeling. It is important to use high-quality fish oil supplements formulated for pets and to consult a veterinarian before starting, as high doses can cause gastrointestinal upset or interfere with blood clotting.
