From Sloppy Sit to Strong Foundation: A Dog's Journey

joint 2026-07-16

A common scenario in veterinary rehabilitation is the dog with a 'sloppy sit'—sliding one or both hind legs out to the side when sitting. This is often an early sign of joint discomfort or weakness. Through a consistent program of tidy-sit exercises (using a wall for support) and gentle weight-shifting, owners can help their dogs build the postural muscles needed for correct alignment. Many dogs actually enjoy these focused sessions. The payoff is a noticeable improvement in their ease of movement and a stronger, more stable body, reducing their long-term injury risk.
