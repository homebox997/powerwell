A Cavalier King Charles Spaniel's Journey Through Stage B2

heart 2026-07-18

Charlie, a 9-year-old Cavalier King Charles Spaniel, was diagnosed with MMVD during a routine checkup despite showing no signs of illness. An echocardiogram revealed his left heart chambers were enlarged, placing him in stage B2. His owner was worried about sudden heart problems, but his veterinarian explained that many dogs in this stage remain stable. Charlie started on pimobendan and continued regular monitoring. Over the next year, his electrocardiograms showed only minor P wave changes without dangerous arrhythmias. Charlie maintained his energy and quality of life, illustrating that stage B2 does not always mean imminent heart failure.
