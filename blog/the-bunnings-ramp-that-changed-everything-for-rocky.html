The Bunnings Ramp That Changed Everything for Rocky

Tips & Guides · Published July 09, 2026 · 2 min read

---

Rocky is 12 years old with arthritic hips. Getting on and off the couch used to mean a struggle—or me lifting his 32kg frame every time. Then I built a simple ramp from a piece of plywood and non-slip rubber matting from Bunnings. Total cost: $30. Total time: 20 minutes.

That one change sparked a full home safety overhaul. Here's what I learned about making a home truly senior-dog friendly.

Key Senior Dog Home Adjustments

Small tweaks can cut slip risks, ease joint strain, and boost daily comfort for your senior pup. Here's what matters most:

1. Clear walkways (no clutter to trip over)

Senior dogs often have reduced vision and slower reaction times. Keep paths to food, water, and toilet areas obstacle-free.

2. Swap for an orthopedic bed

Supports hips and spine—critical for achy joints. Rocky now sleeps through the night without shifting constantly.

3. Bed placement: Quiet, temperature-stable spots

Avoid AC/heating blasts. Older dogs struggle more with temperature regulation.

4. Raise food/water bowls

Eases neck strain for larger or stiff dogs. For Rocky, a 15cm lift meant no more "dropping" his head heavily.

5. Add night lights

Fights vision loss in dark hallways/bathrooms. Plugged a cheap LED sensor light near Rocky's bed—no more midnight bumps.

6. Mark glass doors

Prevents collisions. A simple sticker strip at dog eye-level works.

7. Easy toilet access

Keep pads/bags close; dry wet paws post-outdoor trips to prevent slips on indoor floors.

8. Boost floor grip: Non-slip rugs/mats on timber/tile

Focus on bed, food, and doorway zones. I used Bunnings' anti-slip matting—same stuff as the ramp.

9. Check mobility quarterly

Update setup if stiffness/slipping starts. What worked at age 10 may not work at 12.

FAQ: Senior Dog Home Safety

Most important change?

Fix slippery floors—they're the top risk for arthritic/weak-legged seniors. One non-slip mat can prevent a costly injury.

Ramps vs. stairs?

Ramps win. They cut joint strain (no jumping) for furniture/car access. For Rocky, the $30 Bunnings ramp meant independence—he can now get on the couch whenever he wants, without waiting for me.

Orthopedic beds necessary?

Yes, for most seniors. They reduce pressure points, making rest more comfortable. Watch for signs of stiffness after lying down—that's your cue.

Raised bowls?

Useful for large/stiff dogs. Ask your vet for height guidance. Rule of thumb: bowl should be at or just below elbow height.

How often to update the home setup?

Every 3 months (or sooner if mobility worsens). Rocky's needs changed noticeably between age 11 and 12.

Final Thoughts (Australian Pet Owners)

You don't need a budget overhaul. A $30 ramp, a $20 non-slip mat, a supportive bed, or a raised bowl can transform your senior dog's days. The goal? A home where they move easily, rest comfortably, and feel secure.

After all, they've walked beside you for years—now let your space walk with them.

This is educational—if your dog slips, limps, or shows pain, consult an Australian vet immediately.
