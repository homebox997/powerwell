Case Study: A 10-Year-Old Cavalier with CMVD

heart 2026-07-21

A 10-year-old Cavalier King Charles Spaniel presented with cough, exercise intolerance, and an enlarged left atrium (LA/Ao ratio > 1.7) on echocardiography. The dog was diagnosed with chronic mitral valve disease (CMVD) stage D heart failure. Despite treatment with diuretics and pimobendan, the dog's condition deteriorated over six months. A post-mortem examination confirmed severe mitral valve lesions (type 4) and left atrial enlargement. Histopathology revealed significant perivascular fibrosis and vessel narrowing, consistent with CMVD findings from the study. This case illustrates the progressive nature of CMVD in small breeds.
