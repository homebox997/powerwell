Left Atrial Enlargement in Canine Heart Disease

heart 2026-07-21

In dogs, both dilated cardiomyopathy (DCM) and chronic mitral valve disease (CMVD) lead to enlargement of the left atrium. This condition is detectable via echocardiography, typically measured by the left atrial-to-aortic root diameter ratio (LA/Ao). A ratio greater than 1.7 indicates significant enlargement. Dogs with DCM often also experience rhythm disturbances like atrial fibrillation, while those with CMVD may have fewer arrhythmias. Symptoms of heart failure, such as coughing, difficulty breathing, and exercise intolerance, can accompany this enlargement as the disease progresses to advanced stages.
