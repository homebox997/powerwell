OA is Underdiagnosed Despite High Prevalence

joint 2026-07-16

Canine osteoarthritis is one of the most common causes of chronic pain in dogs, yet it remains significantly underdiagnosed. Many dogs only receive a diagnosis once their mobility is severely impaired. Using diagnostic tools like orthopedic exams and imaging can confirm the presence and severity of joint disease. However, not all mobility issues are OA-related; neurologic or other conditions may mimic OA and require different treatment.
