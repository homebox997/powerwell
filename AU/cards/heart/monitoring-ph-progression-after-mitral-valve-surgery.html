Monitoring PH Progression After Mitral Valve Surgery

heart 2026-07-18

After mitral valve repair (MVR) surgery for dogs with pulmonary hypertension (PH), regular monitoring is essential for detecting residual or recurrent PH. This study recommends echocardiographic follow-up at 3 months post-surgery and again at 6-24 months. Key parameters to monitor include tricuspid regurgitation velocity, left atrial to aortic ratio (LA/Ao), and right ventricular function. Owners should watch for recurrence of clinical signs such as coughing, lethargy, or breathing difficulties. Early detection of PH recurrence allows for timely medical management, potentially including vasodilators or other PH-specific therapies to maintain quality of life.
