Warning: Overweight Dogs with Heartworm Face Higher Pulmonary Hypertension Risk

heart 2026-07-21

The 2023 study found that heartworm-infected dogs with low body condition scores (underweight) had significantly higher NT-proBNP concentrations, indicating more severe pulmonary hypertension. However, obesity also poses serious risks for heartworm patients. Excess body fat increases cardiovascular workload, worsens inflammation, and can mask weight loss that signals disease progression. Owners should maintain their dog at a healthy, lean body condition throughout heartworm treatment. Avoid free-feeding or high-calorie treats during the restricted-exercise recovery period. Consult your veterinarian for a tailored nutrition plan to support healing without promoting obesity or malnutrition.
