Case Study: A Cavalier King Charles Spaniel with Stage B2 MMVD

heart 2026-07-18

An 8-year-old Cavalier King Charles Spaniel was diagnosed with Stage B2 myxomatous mitral valve disease during a routine health check. The owner noticed no symptoms, but echocardiography showed significant left atrial enlargement. The dog was started on pimobendan monotherapy. After one year of treatment, follow-up scans revealed reduced heart dimensions and stable mitral regurgitation. The dog remained active and asymptomatic, demonstrating the benefit of early intervention in managing this common canine heart condition.
