Myth Busted: Running Doesn't Cause Arthritis in Dogs

heart 2026-07-18

A common misconception is that regular running, especially on hard surfaces, causes arthritis in dogs. Scientific evidence shows that moderate, consistent exercise does not cause joint disease in healthy dogs. In fact, proper exercise strengthens muscles that support joints and improves joint lubrication. The primary risk factors for canine arthritis are genetics, obesity, injury, and developmental disorders like hip dysplasia. However, excessive high-impact exercise on a dog with existing joint laxity or abnormal conformation can accelerate damage.
