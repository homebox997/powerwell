Hot spots keep coming back every summer - predictable pattern

👤 James L. 📅 2026-07-11 🐕 Zeus, German Shepherd 💬 8 comments ❤️ 56 likes 👁 850 views
German Shepherd gets hot spots every summer. Same spot, same time. Seeking proactive prevention tips for the upcoming summer season.
Zeus gets hot spots every single summer without fail. Same spot on his hip, turns into a raw weeping mess within days. We treat it, it heals, next January it comes back. It's become so predictable I almost set a calendar reminder. Vets have checked for allergies but nothing conclusive. They said it might just be heat and humidity related. This summer I want to be more proactive. Does anyone have a prevention protocol that actually works? I hate seeing him in discomfort and I know the steroids can't be good for him long-term.

Gary M. 2026-07-11

GSDs and hot spots are such a vicious cycle because of their thick coats. The key is drying the area completely after every bath and swim — moisture + bacteria = hot spot.

Karen H. 2026-07-11

Manuka honey applied after clipping and cleaning the area has been incredible for our lab's hot spots. Medical grade from the chemist, about $18 for a tube. Worth trying!

Dave T. 2026-07-11

Elizabethan collars (the cone) are annoying but essential. Our staffy kept re-opening the hot spot by licking. 14 days in the cone = finally healed.

Rob F. 2026-07-11

Hot spots under the ear and on the neck are often secondary ear infection signs — the dog scratches the ear, bacteria spreads to the skin nearby. Check the ears too!

Brett C. 2026-07-11

The seasonal pattern suggests environmental allergens. Our beagle's hot spots only appear in summer. Our vet recommended Apoquel again which breaks the allergy-itch cycle.

Tracy L. 2026-07-11

Clipping the hair around the hot spot is non-negotiable — can't treat it properly through a thick coat. Vet can do it or we do it at home with clippers.

Sue P. 2026-07-11

Does anyone know if probiotics help with recurring hot spots? Our kelpie seems to get them every summer and I wonder if it's gut-immune related.

Nina B. 2026-07-11

We switched to an antimicrobial shampoo from the vet (Chlorhexidine based) and the recurrence rate dropped from every 6 weeks to maybe once a year. The cheap pet shop stuff just doesn't cut it.
