Key Electrocardiographic Changes in Stage B2 Dogs

heart 2026-07-18

A study comparing 60 dogs with MMVD in stages B1 and B2 found statistically significant increases in P wave maximum duration (Pmax), P wave minimum duration (Pmin), and short-term instability (STI) in stage B2 dogs. However, no significant differences were observed in heart rate variability (HRV), P wave dispersion, or QT interval dispersion between the two groups. These findings suggest that while cardiac remodeling occurs in stage B2, it does not dramatically alter the sympathovagal balance or strongly predispose dogs to arrhythmias compared to stage B1.
