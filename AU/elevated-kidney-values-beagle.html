Blood test showed elevated kidney values - terrified

👤 Peter H. 📅 2026-07-11 🐕 Buddy, Beagle 💬 8 comments ❤️ 160 likes 👁 2620 views
Buddy's blood test showed Stage 2 kidney disease. 11-year-old Beagle still seems fine in himself. Terrified owner seeking support and advice.
Buddy had his annual blood test last week and the vet called me back about his kidney values. Creatinine and BUN both elevated. She mentioned something about Stage 2 kidney disease. I did NOT handle this news well. Cried in the car for 20 minutes after the call. She's recommended a kidney support diet and wants to re-test in a month. Has anyone been through this? What's the progression like? How long did your dog manage with early-stage kidney disease? Buddy still seems fine in himself - eating, drinking, happy. I keep looking at him and trying not to fall apart.

Brett C. 2026-07-11

I know this feeling exactly. Our beagle Max had elevated BUN and creatinine at his 8yo checkup. We were terrified but the vet said catching it early makes a massive difference. Diet change was the first step.

Dave T. 2026-07-11

Please get a UPC (urine protein creatinine) ratio test done as well. It tells you how much protein is leaking through the kidneys. Our staffy had borderline values but the UPC showed it was more serious than we thought.

Tracy L. 2026-07-11

Kidney disease management is all about reducing the kidney's workload. Low phosphorus diet is number one priority. Royal Canin Renal or Hill's k/d are the gold standard — your vet should have samples.

Karen H. 2026-07-11

How much does your beagle drink? Increased thirst is often the first sign pet parents notice. We measured Murphy's water intake for a week and he was drinking nearly double what he used to.

Rob F. 2026-07-11

Royal Canin Renal is actually quite palatable compared to other kidney diets. Max took to it without issues. Hill's k/d is the other one vets seem to prefer.

Nina B. 2026-07-11

Don't lose hope. Our poodle was diagnosed stage 2 at 10 and lived to 13 with proper management. It's about quality of life, not just quantity.

Mick R. 2026-07-11

Has anyone tried the Subcutaneous fluid therapy at home for advanced kidney disease? Our vet suggested it and it scared us but apparently it's routine for some owners.

Gary M. 2026-07-11

Please ask your vet about ACE inhibitors — they can help reduce protein loss through the kidneys. Ours has been on them for 18 months and her values have stabilized.
