Shelter Dog's Elevated NT-proBNP Revealed Hidden Heartworm Danger

heart 2026-07-21

A 7-year-old mixed-breed shelter dog presented for routine adoption screening in a heartworm-endemic region. The dog appeared healthy with no cough or exercise intolerance, but a heartworm antigen test was positive. Routine bloodwork revealed an NT-proBNP concentration of 1,450 pmol/L, well above the 1,178.45 pmol/L threshold. An echocardiogram confirmed moderate pulmonary hypertension (RPAD Index 27%). Thanks to this biomarker, the veterinary team implemented strict cage rest and started sildenafil before adulticide therapy, preventing potential life-threatening complications. The dog successfully completed treatment and was adopted into a loving home.
