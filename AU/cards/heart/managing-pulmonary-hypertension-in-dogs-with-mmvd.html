Managing Pulmonary Hypertension in Dogs with MMVD

heart 2026-07-20

Pulmonary hypertension (high blood pressure in the lungs) is a common complication of advanced myxomatous mitral valve disease (MMVD) in dogs. It can worsen coughing, breathing difficulties, and fainting episodes. Treatment options include sildenafil, a medication that dilates pulmonary blood vessels, and pimobendan, which also helps reduce pulmonary pressure. Oxygen therapy and managing the underlying heart failure are also important. The ACVIM panel recommends that dogs with MMVD and suspected pulmonary hypertension undergo echocardiography to confirm the diagnosis and guide targeted therapy for better outcomes.
