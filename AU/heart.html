Heart Disease in Senior Dogs: Complete Guide 2026

Heart disease affects up to 10 percent of all dogs in Australia. This complete guide covers symptoms, severity levels, treatment options, lifestyle changes and real community case studies — everything you need to help your dog live comfortably.

💡 Key Takeaway

Heart disease is common but manageable for your dog's quality of life. With early diagnosis, proper medication, and lifestyle adjustments, most senior dogs with arthritis can live active, happy lives for years after diagnosis.

Types of Heart Disease in Dogs?

Heart disease in dogs takes several forms where the protective cartilage in joints wears down over time, causing pain, stiffness, and reduced mobility. Degenerative Valve Disease (MVD) is most common in small-to-medium breeds (Cavalier KC, Poodles, Chihuahuas). The heart valves wear out and leak blood. Dilated Cardiomyopathy (DCM) affects large breeds (Dobermans, Boxers, Great Danes). The heart muscle weakens. Heart Murmurs are abnormal sounds during heartbeat cycles and should always be evaluated.

Heart disease is especially common in senior dogs over 7 years old. 75 percent of senior dogs develop some form of heart murmur. Annual vet heart checks are essential for early detection. While most forms cannot be cured, they can be managed effectively. Early diagnosis is critical. The earlier treatment begins, the better the long-term outcome.

🦡🐕
Arthritis most commonly affects hips, elbows, knees and spine in senior dogs. Early signs are often subtle — look for stiffness after rest.

Heart Disease Care and Treatment

Regular Vet Checkups and Scans

Annual heart checks (stethoscope exam, X-rays. Even a 5% reduction in body weight can significantly reduce pain in arthritic dogs. Work with your vet to find the ideal weight for your dog's breed and size.

Heart Medication

ACE inhibitors, diuretics, inotropes, omega-3 fish oil and green-lipped mussel can help Never adjust medication without vet consultation. Consistent daily dosing is essential — supplements work best when given daily without skipping doses.

Controlled Exercise

Short, gentle walks are better than long exercise and reduce pressure points. Keep bedding warm — cold weather worsens stiffness. Avoid hot weather and strenuous activity.

Low-Sodium Diet

Keep Calm and Comfortable

Stress worsens heart symptoms, non-slip rugs on slippery floors, raised food bowls, and easy access to water can all reduce joint stress. A calm, stable home makes a real difference a dog's quality of life.
