Signs of Cardiogenic Pulmonary Edema in Dogs with MMVD

heart 2026-07-18

Dogs with myxomatous mitral valve disease (MMVD) who develop cardiogenic pulmonary edema (CPE) often present with a high respiratory rate and increased breathing effort. In a study of 100 hospitalized dogs, the median respiratory rate upon presentation was 64 breaths per minute. Breathing difficulty is quantified using the Tufts Dyspnea Score (TDS), which ranges from 0 to 10; the median TDS in the study was 5. Other signs may include coughing, restlessness, and reluctance to lie down, indicating fluid buildup in the lungs due to heart failure.
