Mitral Valve Repair as Treatment for PH in Dogs

heart 2026-07-18

Mitral valve repair (MVR) surgery under cardiopulmonary bypass is a treatment option for dogs with myxomatous mitral valve disease (MMVD) complicated by pulmonary hypertension (PH). This study of 21 dogs showed that MVR significantly reduces estimated pulmonary arterial pressure and improves heart function. After surgery, echocardiographic parameters such as normalized left ventricular internal diameter in diastole (LVIDDN), mitral E wave velocity, left atrial to aortic ratio (LA/Ao), and tricuspid regurgitation velocity all significantly decreased (p < 0.01). Most dogs achieved complete resolution of clinical signs, though some required ongoing monitoring for residual or recurrent PH.
