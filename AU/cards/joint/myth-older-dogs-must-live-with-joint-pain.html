Myth: Older Dogs Must Live with Joint Pain

joint 2026-07-16

A common misconception is that joint pain is an unavoidable part of aging in dogs. While osteoarthritis is more common in senior dogs, it is not something owners should accept without action. Modern veterinary care offers many effective treatments: prescription pain relievers, joint supplements (e.g., fish oil, glucosamine, ASU), weight management, physical therapy, and even acupuncture. The 2025 clinical study shows that dietary supplements can significantly reduce pain and improve mobility. Ignoring pain worsens muscle weakness and joint damage. Always consult a veterinarian to create a tailored pain management plan—your dog doesn't have to suffer silently.

Tags: myth, aging, dog pain, misconception, treatment options
Source: Frontiers | A randomized, double-blind, controlled study on the efficacy of an oral dietary supplement containing fish oil, ASU and phytotherapeutic extracts in canine osteoarthritis
