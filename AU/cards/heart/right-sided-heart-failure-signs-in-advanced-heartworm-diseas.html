Right-Sided Heart Failure Signs in Advanced Heartworm Disease

heart 2026-07-21

As heartworm disease progresses, pulmonary hypertension can lead to right-sided congestive heart failure. Key signs include ascites (fluid accumulation in the abdomen), jugular venous distension (visible swelling of the jugular vein), and hepatomegaly (enlarged liver). These symptoms indicate severe strain on the right side of the heart. In the study, dogs with these signs had elevated NT-proBNP concentrations. Prompt veterinary attention is critical, as right-sided heart failure is a life-threatening condition that requires immediate medical management, including diuretics and oxygen therapy.
