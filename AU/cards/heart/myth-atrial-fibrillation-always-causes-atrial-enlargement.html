Myth: Atrial Fibrillation Always Causes Atrial Enlargement

heart 2026-07-21

A common misconception is that atrial fibrillation (AF) is always the cause of left atrial enlargement in dogs with dilated cardiomyopathy (DCM). However, the relationship is not clear-cut. While DCM is often accompanied by AF, it remains uncertain whether AF leads to atrial enlargement or is a result of it. The study notes that there is no consensus on this cause-effect relationship. In chronic mitral valve disease (CMVD), arrhythmias like AF are observed less frequently, yet left atrial enlargement still occurs due to volume overload. Therefore, both conditions can cause atrial enlargement independently of rhythm disturbances.
