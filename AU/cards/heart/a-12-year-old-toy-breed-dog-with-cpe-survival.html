A 12-Year-Old Toy Breed Dog with CPE Survival

heart 2026-07-18

A 12-year-old, 3.3 kg male dog with myxomatous mitral valve disease (MMVD) was hospitalized for severe breathing difficulty (Tufts Dyspnea Score of 7). On arrival, his respiratory rate was 68 breaths per minute. He received oxygen therapy and injectable medications. Within 12 hours, his dyspnea score dropped to 3, and his respiratory rate stabilized to 40 breaths per minute. He was discharged after 22 hours of oxygen. However, two months later, he was re-hospitalized for another episode of pulmonary edema, illustrating the risk of recurrence even after successful initial treatment.
