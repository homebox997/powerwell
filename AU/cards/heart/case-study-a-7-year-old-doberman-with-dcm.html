Case Study: A 7-Year-Old Doberman with DCM

heart 2026-07-21

A 7-year-old Doberman Pinscher was diagnosed with dilated cardiomyopathy (DCM) after presenting with weakness, fainting, and an irregular heartbeat. Echocardiography showed a dilated left ventricle, decreased fractional shortening (FS < 20%), and left atrial enlargement (LA/Ao > 1.7). The dog was treated for heart failure and arrhythmias but eventually succumbed to refractory disease. Post-mortem analysis revealed attenuated wavy fiber (AWF) type lesions in the left ventricle and extensive interstitial fibrosis in the left atrium, with less vessel narrowing compared to CMVD cases. This highlights the distinct pathological features of DCM.
