Max the Border Collie Returns to Agility After Surgery

joint 2026-07-16

Max, a 5-year-old Border Collie, developed sudden hindlimb lameness after an agility competition. His owner noticed swelling behind the right hock and a tendency to skip on that leg. Veterinary examination and ultrasound confirmed a partial gastrocnemius tendon tear. Max underwent the novel minimally invasive tarsal stabilization procedure. Within 6 weeks, he was walking comfortably and gradually returned to controlled exercise. By 12 weeks post-op, he was back to light agility training. His owner reported Max had no visible limp and seemed comfortable. Max continues to compete successfully, with no recurrence after 18 months.
