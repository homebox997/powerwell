Warning: Diuretics Can Cause Dangerous Electrolyte Shifts

heart 2026-07-19

Diuretics are commonly used in dogs with congestive heart failure, but they carry risks. They can cause hypokalemia (low potassium) due to increased renal loss, especially with high aldosterone levels. This may lead to muscle weakness, cardiac arrhythmias, or sudden death. Also, excessive diuresis can worsen hyponatremia (low sodium). Regular blood electrolyte monitoring is essential during therapy. Never adjust diuretic doses without veterinary guidance.
