Oatmeal baths for itchy dogs - does it actually work?

👤 Danielle P. 📅 2026-07-11 🐕 Poppy, Labrador 💬 8 comments ❤️ 67 likes 👁 1260 views
Regular oatmeal baths for Poppy's itchy skin. Seems to help but is there science behind it? Seeking evidence-based perspective on home remedies.
My mum swears by oatmeal baths for Poppy's itchy skin. She makes this whole paste from colloidal oatmeal and I honestly feel ridiculous doing it but Poppy seems to enjoy the bath and does seem a bit more comfortable after. Partner thinks I'm wasting my time. Is there any actual evidence it helps or is this just a nice bonding ritual? Not going to stop either way but curious what the science says.

8 Comments

Dave T. 2026-07-11

Oatmeal baths genuinely work for mild itchy skin — colloidal oatmeal from the chemist (about $12) blended to powder in a blender and added to warm bath water. 10-15 mins soak then pat dry.

Tracy L. 2026-07-11

We add a tablespoon of coconut oil to the oatmeal bath for extra moisturising. Our golden's coat is so soft afterwards. The combination is chef's kiss.

Brett C. 2026-07-11

Plain rolled oats (not instant) from the supermarket work just as well as the fancy colloidal oatmeal products. Whiz them up in a food processor until fine.

Karen H. 2026-07-11

For our lab's hotspot specifically, the vet recommended Domeboro (aluminum acetate) soaks mixed with the oatmeal. Sounds fancy but it's like $8 from the chemist.

Rob F. 2026-07-11

Don't use human oatmeal body washes — they have fragrances and additives that can make itching worse! Plain colloidal oatmeal only.

Gary M. 2026-07-11

Our GSD's oatmeal bath routine: lukewarm water, 20 min soak, no shampoo, pat dry, then applying a thin layer of coconut oil while still slightly damp. Coat looks amazing the next day.

Nina B. 2026-07-11

We do oatmeal baths twice a week during summer when the scratching is worst. Plus we freeze a Kong with peanut butter and give it to her IN the bath so she stays calm and distracted.

Sue P. 2026-07-11

Oatmeal baths are great for soothing but they're not a cure for underlying allergies. Still worth getting the allergy test done if the scratching is persistent.
