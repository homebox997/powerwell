PH Recurrence: A Late Complication After Surgery

heart 2026-07-18

Even after successful mitral valve repair (MVR) surgery, some dogs may experience late recurrence of pulmonary hypertension (PH). In this study, 14.2% of dogs that initially had resolution of PH postoperatively developed recurrent PH at later follow-up visits (6-24 months after surgery). This suggests that some dogs may have underlying pulmonary arterial pathology that persists even after correcting the left heart problem. Owners should remain vigilant for returning signs such as coughing, exercise intolerance, or breathing difficulties after surgery. Regular echocardiographic monitoring is recommended to detect PH recurrence early and adjust treatment accordingly.
