Heartworm Warning Signs: From Mild Cough to Collapse

heart 2026-07-21

Dogs infected with heartworm (Dirofilaria immitis) may remain asymptomatic in mild cases, but as the disease progresses, classic symptoms appear. These include a persistent mild cough, exercise intolerance, weakness, weight loss, dyspnea (difficulty breathing), and syncope (fainting). In advanced stages with right-sided congestive heart failure, owners may also observe ascites (fluid buildup in the abdomen), jugular venous distension, and hepatomegaly (enlarged liver). Any combination of these signs warrants immediate veterinary evaluation, especially in regions where heartworm is endemic.
