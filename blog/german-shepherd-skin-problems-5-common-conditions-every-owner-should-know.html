German Shepherd Skin Problems: 5 Common Conditions Every Owner Should Know

Health Case · Published July 09, 2026 · 3 min read

---

German Shepherds are one of Australia's most beloved breeds — admired for their intelligence, loyalty, and striking looks. But that thick double coat comes with a downside: it makes them especially prone to skin conditions that can cause real discomfort and, left untreated, lead to more serious health problems.

Here are the five most common skin problems affecting German Shepherds, and what you can do about each one.

1. Allergic Dermatitis

German Shepherds are frequent sufferers of allergic reactions, often triggered by environmental factors like pollen, dust mites, or mould — or by food ingredients such as beef, dairy, or grains. Symptoms include intense itching, redness, inflammation, and recurring ear infections. Constant scratching can open the door to secondary bacterial or yeast infections, making things worse.

What to do: Work with your vet to pinpoint the trigger through an elimination diet or allergy testing. Antihistamines, omega-3 supplements, and medicated shampoos can help keep symptoms under control.

2. Pyoderma

This bacterial skin infection is common in German Shepherds, particularly those with thick coats that trap moisture. It shows up as pustules, crusts, or red, weeping lesions — usually on the belly, armpits, or between the toes. Causes range from allergies and flea bites to skin folds that create a damp environment where bacteria (such as Staphylococcus) thrive.

What to do: Treatment means antibiotics prescribed by your vet, topical antiseptic washes, and tackling the underlying trigger (allergies or parasites) to stop it coming back.

3. Seborrhea

Seborrhea is caused by abnormal sebum production, leading to either dry, flaky skin (dry seborrhea) or greasy, oily patches with a strong odour (oily seborrhea). It can be primary (inherited) or secondary to conditions like hypothyroidism, allergies, or parasites.

What to do: Regular baths with a medicated shampoo, plus treatment of any underlying condition, keep symptoms manageable. Your vet will recommend the right shampoo for dry versus oily seborrhea.

4. Mange

Caused by mites, mange affects German Shepherds in two forms. Sarcoptic mange (scabies) causes severe itching, hair loss, and crusting, and spreads fast to other pets — and people. Demodectic mange, linked to a weakened immune system, leads to localised or widespread hair loss, often in puppies.

What to do: A veterinary diagnosis via skin scraping is essential. Treatment uses anti-parasitic medication, alongside addressing any underlying immune issues, to support recovery.

5. Flea and Tick Infestations

These parasites cause intense itching and skin irritation. Flea saliva can trigger an allergic reaction (flea allergy dermatitis), leading to redness, hair loss, and scabs. Ticks transmit disease and can cause localised swelling.

What to do: Prevention is everything — topical treatments, collars, or oral medications, plus regular grooming to catch infestations early. For Australian owners, year-round prevention matters even more in our warm climate.

Prevention Tips for German Shepherd Owners

That thick double coat needs regular upkeep to keep skin problems at bay. Brush your dog at least twice a week, bathe only when needed with a gentle dog shampoo, and keep their living space clean. Above all, keep up with routine vet check-ups — early detection is the key to managing these conditions well.

If you notice persistent scratching, hair loss, or skin changes in your German Shepherd, don't wait. Book a vet appointment promptly — your dog's comfort depends on catching these issues early.
