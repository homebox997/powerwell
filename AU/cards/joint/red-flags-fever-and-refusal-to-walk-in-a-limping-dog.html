Red Flags: Fever and Refusal to Walk in a Limping Dog

joint 2026-07-16

When a dog is limping, certain signs indicate a more serious underlying condition requiring urgent veterinary care. A fever above 38.5°C (101.3°F), a high white blood cell count, elevated ESR or CRP, and refusal to walk are red flags for septic arthritis (a joint infection). Dogs with septic arthritis often look clinically 'toxic' or very sick. Other serious causes include osteomyelitis (bone infection) or Lyme disease, which can also cause lameness. If your limping dog has a fever or seems lethargic, seek emergency vet attention immediately.
