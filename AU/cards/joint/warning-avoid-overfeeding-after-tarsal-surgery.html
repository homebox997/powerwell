Warning: Avoid Overfeeding After Tarsal Surgery

joint 2026-07-16

After tarsal stabilization surgery, it is critical to prevent weight gain. Excess body weight increases stress on the repaired tendon and the hock joint, risking re-injury or implant failure. Owners should strictly follow post-operative feeding guidelines, reducing calorie intake by 20-30% if the dog is less active during recovery. Avoid high-fat treats and table scraps. Use measured portions and consider a weight management diet if needed. Obesity can delay healing, increase the risk of arthritis in the affected joint, and compromise the surgical outcome. Work with your veterinarian to maintain an ideal body condition score throughout the recovery period.
