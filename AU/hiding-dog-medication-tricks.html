Tips for giving a senior dog their medication disguised in food?

👤 Helen C. 📅 2026-07-11 🐕 Molly, Cavalier King Charles Spaniel 💬 8 comments ❤️ 134 likes 👁 1370 views
10-year-old CKCS has figured out every medication hiding trick. What's worked for your clever stubborn senior dog? Need fresh ideas.
Molly needs to take heart medication twice a day and she's absolutely onto me. Wrapping it in cheese - works for exactly 2 days then she spits it out. Peanut butter - same. Pill pockets from the pet shop - she figured those out too. She now eyes every treat with suspicion. It's like living with a tiny furry detective. What are your tricks? I need variety because she's learning fast. What's worked for your stubborn senior dog?

8 Comments

Dave T. 2026-07-11

Cheese is the classic but my staffy figured it out after 3 days lol. Now we use Pyranha Pill Wrap (the moldable paste) and he has no idea. $14 from the vet.

Karen H. 2026-07-11

Hot dog sausages (cheap ones from the butcher) — our lab takes any pill hidden in a slice of hot dog and she's too excited to taste the pill. Game changer for us.

Gary M. 2026-07-11

Pill crushers from the chemist are amazing. We crush our GSD's meds and mix into a tablespoon of mashed sweet potato. Can't even taste it.

Rob F. 2026-07-11

The Greenies Pill Pocket brand works well for capsules but not as well for bitter-tasting tablets. Our border collie rejected the bitter ones through everything until we tried Pill Pockets.

Brett C. 2026-07-11

We use liverwurst from the deli — it masks absolutely everything and most dogs lose their minds over it. Downside is the sodium so we use it sparingly.

Nina B. 2026-07-11

A bit of peanut butter (xylitol-free!) inside a piece of soft bread, wrapped tight around the pill. Our poodle thinks it's a treat every time.

Tracy L. 2026-07-11

Has anyone tried the Pill Gun (plastic syringe thing)? We use it for our golden's slippery capsules — quick plunger and it's in the back of the throat before she knows it.

Mick R. 2026-07-11

Treat training with dummy pills first (no meds) for a week builds the association. By the time the real meds come, the dog is conditioned to take the treat without suspicion.
