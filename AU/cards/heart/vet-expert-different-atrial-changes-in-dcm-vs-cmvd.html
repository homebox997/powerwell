Vet Expert: Different Atrial Changes in DCM vs. CMVD

heart 2026-07-21

According to veterinary pathologists, the histopathologic changes in the left atrium differ significantly between dogs with dilated cardiomyopathy (DCM) and chronic mitral valve disease (CMVD). In DCM, the atria show more interstitial fibrosis and severe cardiomyocyte degeneration, suggesting a primary myocardial disease. In CMVD, the atria exhibit more perivascular fibrosis and vessel narrowing, likely due to chronic volume overload from mitral regurgitation. These differences imply that the mechanisms of atrial remodeling are distinct, which may influence treatment approaches and prognosis for each disease.
