Warning: Do Not Withhold Diuretics During MMVD Treatment

heart 2026-07-20

A critical feeding and medication risk for dogs with myxomatous mitral valve disease (MMVD) is the misuse of diuretics like furosemide. Owners should never abruptly stop or reduce diuretic doses without veterinary guidance, as this can rapidly trigger fluid buildup in the lungs, leading to a life-threatening crisis. Conversely, over-diuresis can cause dehydration, kidney damage, and electrolyte imbalances. Additionally, avoid giving high-sodium treats or table scraps, which can worsen fluid retention. Always follow your vet's prescription precisely and monitor for signs of dehydration or worsening heart failure.
