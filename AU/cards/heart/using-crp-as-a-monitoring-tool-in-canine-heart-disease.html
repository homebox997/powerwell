Using CRP as a Monitoring Tool in Canine Heart Disease

heart 2026-07-19

C-reactive protein (CRP) is a major acute-phase protein in dogs that increases with inflammation. While not specific, CRP can aid in monitoring heart disease. In dogs with dilated cardiomyopathy (DCM), CRP levels may rise even before lung changes are seen on X-rays or echocardiograms. It can also indicate pulmonary hypertension or endothelial arteritis, especially in heartworm disease. Measuring CRP every 24-48 hours after a cardiac event helps track inflammation, guiding treatment adjustments and prognosis.
