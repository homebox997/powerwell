Drinking heaps more water lately - should I be worried?

👤 Chris J. 📅 2026-07-11 🐕 Cooper, Border Collie 💬 8 comments ❤️ 199 likes 👁 2580 views
Cooper drinking twice as much water as before. 9-year-old Border Collie. Possible kidney, diabetes or Cushings. Vet appointment booked.
Noticed Cooper's been drinking way more water than usual over the past month or so. Like, I have to fill his bowl twice a day now when he used to barely finish it once. Also peeing more frequently obviously. He's a 9-year-old Border Collie, still working him lightly but now I'm paranoid. Read online this could be kidneys, could be diabetes, could be Cushings. All sound terrifying. Vet appointment next week but in the meantime has anyone experienced this with their senior collie?

Rob F. 2026-07-11

Increased thirst can mean kidney OR liver issues OR even Cushing's disease in older dogs. Worth getting a full senior blood panel plus abdominal ultrasound to rule things out systematically.

Brett C. 2026-07-11

Our beagle started drinking heaps and we thought 'good, he's staying hydrated' — turns out it was diabetes. Thank god we went to the vet before it got serious. Never ignore the thirst!

Gary M. 2026-07-11

Border collies are athletes — if Scout is drinking more but still energetic that's actually a good sign vs if he's also lethargic. Any other symptoms to note?

Karen H. 2026-07-11

The water bowl measuring trick saved us. Fill it to a line each morning, measure what's left at night. That gives your vet accurate info. We were way off on our estimates!

Dave T. 2026-07-11

Also worth checking teeth — dental disease can cause increased thirst too. Our staffy's teeth were terrible at 9 and once we got them sorted her water intake went back to normal.

Tracy L. 2026-07-11

Cushing's was our dog's diagnosis after the thirst investigation. It's manageable with medication ($45/month for us). Not ideal but totally liveable.

Nina B. 2026-07-11

Diabetes signs are thirst + weight loss despite eating heaps + cloudy eyes. If Scout has any of those, get blood glucose checked ASAP. But probably something simpler — try not to catastrophize!

Sue P. 2026-07-11

Our kelpie had a false alarm — turned out to be the new high-sodium treats we were giving her. Check what Scout's been eating lately!
