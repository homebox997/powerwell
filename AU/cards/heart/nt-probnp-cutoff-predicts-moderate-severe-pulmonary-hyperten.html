NT-proBNP Cutoff Predicts Moderate-Severe Pulmonary Hypertension

heart 2026-07-21

In a 2023 study of 50 heartworm-infected dogs, 40% were found to have moderate-to-severe pulmonary hypertension (PH) based on echocardiographic criteria (RPAD Index < 29.5%). Researchers identified a serum NT-proBNP cutoff of ≥ 1178.45 pmol/L as a strong predictor of moderate-severe PH. This cutoff yielded a sensitivity of 64.3% and a specificity of 95.5%, meaning it is highly reliable for ruling in PH when elevated, though some cases may be missed. These findings support NT-proBNP as a useful non-invasive biomarker for staging heartworm disease severity.
