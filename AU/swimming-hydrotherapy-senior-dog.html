Swimming for senior dogs - game changer or waste of time?

👤 Amy W. 📅 2026-07-11 🐕 Ruby, German Shepherd 💬 8 comments ❤️ 195 likes 👁 1790 views
Vet physio recommended hydrotherapy for Ruby's hip issues. German Shepherd, 9 years old. Anyone tried dog swimming for joint health?
My vet physio suggested hydrotherapy for Ruby's joints and I'm genuinely curious if it actually makes a difference. Ruby's 9 GSD, hips aren't great. She's not in pain day-to-day but she struggles after long walks and her gait has definitely changed over the last year. There's a hydrotherapy centre near me that does dog swimming sessions. Has anyone taken their senior dog swimming? Did you notice improvement in mobility or was it more about comfort? Not trying to spend money on something that won't help but if it genuinely makes a difference for her quality of life I'll do it in a heartbeat.

Dave T. 2026-07-11

Absolute game changer for our staffy. She hated the water for the first 2 sessions but the hydrotherapist was so patient. By session 4 she was pulling to get in the pool. Her back legs are so much stronger now.

Tracy L. 2026-07-11

Swimming is low impact which is perfect for OA dogs. Our golden does underwater treadmill at our local vet clinic — $65 a session but the results speak for themselves.

Karen H. 2026-07-11

Be careful with cold water temps! My lab came out stiffer after swimming in the ocean in winter. The heated hydrotherapy pools are definitely better for arthritic dogs.

Rob F. 2026-07-11

Our border collie hated being wet but loved the underwater treadmill because she could still 'run' without the full body immersion. Might be a good middle ground if your dog is water-shy.

Gary M. 2026-07-11

Hydrotherapy is magic for post-op recovery too. Our GSD had TPLO surgery and the rehab swimming schedule got her back to normal way faster than the vet expected.

Mick R. 2026-07-11

Jen W. 2026-07-11

Search for 'AHAA hydrotherapy Australia' — the Australian Hydrotherapy Association has a directory. Saved us so much time calling around.

Brett C. 2026-07-11

We bought an inflatable pool from BCF for $89 and set it up in the backyard. Heated with a pond heater during winter. Not the same as a proper pool but it's something!
