My old mate is on 4 medications now - anyone else managing this?

👤 Rob W. 📅 2026-07-11 🐕 Harley, Staffordshire Bull Terrier 💬 8 comments ❤️ 98 likes 👁 1830 views
12-year-old Staffy on 4 different medications daily. Exhausted owner seeking tips for managing multiple medications and medication scheduling.
Harley's 12 and a half. He's on heart medication, a diuretic, something for his kidneys and gabapentin for his back. That's 4 different medications, some twice a day. I feel like I'm running a pharmacy. Scheduling is a nightmare. Does anyone else have their senior dog on multiple medications? How do you manage? Any tips for getting him to take them without a fight? He's doing well all things considered but managing the meds is exhausting. Would love to know I'm not alone in this.

Dave T. 2026-07-11

Four meds sounds overwhelming but our staffy is on 5 now and it's totally manageable. We use a weekly pill organiser from the chemist and it's been a lifesaver.

Karen H. 2026-07-11

Pill pockets from Dine A Chook or even just wrapping in cheese work well. Some meds can't be crushed though so check with your vet first!

Tracy L. 2026-07-11

Our golden's on Gabapentin + Previcox + joint supplement + thyroid meds. The trick is spacing them out through the day so they're not all hitting the stomach at once.

Rob F. 2026-07-11

Have you looked into compounding pharmacies? They can make the meds into liquids or even flavored chews if your dog is hard to pill. Total game changer for us.

Gary M. 2026-07-11

The coordination of meds is so real. We use an alarm on our phone for each dose time. We look ridiculous but the dog is getting everything he needs!

Brett C. 2026-07-11

Be careful with supplement interactions! Our beagle's gabapentin and a herbal calming supplement he was given had an unexpected interaction. Always tell your vet about EVERYTHING you're giving.

Jen W. 2026-07-11

Ask your vet about the 'lowest effective dose' approach — sometimes meds can be reduced as dogs adjust, especially the anti-inflammatories.

Nina B. 2026-07-11

The stress is real for us too. Sometimes I lie awake thinking 'did I give him his 4pm dose or not?' — 6 months later and I'm still not fully confident 😅
