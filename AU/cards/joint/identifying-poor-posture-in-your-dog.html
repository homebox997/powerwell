Identifying Poor Posture in Your Dog

joint 2026-07-16

Poor posture can be a sign of underlying orthopaedic issues in dogs. When your dog is standing, look for: weight shifting off one leg, toes pointing outward or inward, a tucked or roached back (instead of a level topline), a tucked pelvis (lower than the withers), and a head carried lower than the shoulders. In a sitting position, a 'sloppy sit' where the dog's legs slide out to one side or both hips are uneven is a common sign of discomfort. When lying down, watch for a dog that consistently rests with one leg out to the side or struggles to get comfortable. These signs warrant a professional evaluation.
