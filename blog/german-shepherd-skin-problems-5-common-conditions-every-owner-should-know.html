German Shepherd Skin Problems: 5 Common Conditions Every Owner Should Know

Health Case · Published July 09, 2026 · 3 min read

---

German Shepherds are one of the most beloved breeds in Australia, known for their intelligence, loyalty, and striking appearance. But their thick double coat also makes them prone to a range of skin conditions that can cause discomfort and, if left untreated, lead to more serious health issues. Here are five of the most common skin problems affecting this breed and what you can do about them.

1. Allergic Dermatitis

German Shepherds are prone to allergic reactions, often triggered by environmental factors like pollen, dust mites, or mold, or food ingredients such as beef, dairy, or grains. Symptoms include intense itching, redness, inflammation, and recurrent ear infections. Scratching can lead to secondary bacterial or yeast infections, worsening the condition. Identifying the allergen through tests and adjusting diet or environment is key to management.

What to do: Work with your vet to identify triggers through elimination diets or allergy testing. Antihistamines, omega-3 supplements, and medicated shampoos can help manage symptoms.

2. Pyoderma

This bacterial skin infection is common in German Shepherds, especially those with thick coats that trap moisture. It appears as pustules, crusts, or red, oozing lesions, often on the belly, armpits, or between toes. Causes include allergies, flea bites, or skin folds that create a moist environment for bacteria (like Staphylococcus).

What to do: Treatment involves antibiotics prescribed by a veterinarian, topical antiseptic washes, and addressing the underlying triggers such as allergies or parasites to prevent recurrence.

3. Seborrhea

Seborrhea results in abnormal sebum production, leading to dry, flaky skin (dry seborrhea) or greasy, oily patches with a strong odour (oily seborrhea). It may be primary (genetic) or secondary to issues like hypothyroidism, allergies, or parasites.

What to do: Regular bathing with medicated shampoos and treating any underlying conditions helps control symptoms. Your vet can recommend the right shampoo type based on whether the seborrhea is dry or oily.

4. Mange

Caused by mites, mange affects German Shepherds in two forms: sarcoptic (scabies) and demodectic. Sarcoptic mange causes severe itching, hair loss, and crusting, spreading quickly to other pets or humans. Demodectic mange, linked to a weakened immune system, leads to localised or generalised hair loss, often in puppies.

What to do: Veterinary diagnosis via skin scraping is essential. Treatment with anti-parasitic medications, along with addressing any underlying immune issues, is critical for recovery.

5. Flea and Tick Infestations

These parasites cause intense itching and skin irritation. Flea saliva can trigger allergic reactions (flea allergy dermatitis), leading to redness, hair loss, and scabs. Ticks transmit diseases and may cause localised swelling.

What to do: Preventative measures like topical treatments, collars, or oral medications are crucial, along with regular grooming to check for infestations. For Australian owners, year-round prevention is especially important given our warm climate.

Prevention Tips for German Shepherd Owners

Your German Shepherd's thick double coat requires regular care to prevent skin issues from developing. Brush your dog at least twice a week, bathe only when necessary using a gentle dog shampoo, and keep their living environment clean. Most importantly, schedule regular veterinary check-ups — early detection is the key to managing these conditions effectively.

If you notice persistent scratching, hair loss, or skin changes in your German Shepherd, don't wait. Book a vet appointment promptly. Your dog's comfort depends on catching these issues early.
