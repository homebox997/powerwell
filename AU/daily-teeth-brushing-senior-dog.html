How often do you brush your senior dog's teeth? Dentist says daily

👤 Angela M. 📅 2026-07-11 🐕 Tilly, Pomeranian 💬 8 comments ❤️ 141 likes 👁 2200 views
Vet recommends daily teeth brushing for 11-year-old Pomeranian. Dental disease risks discussed. Tips for making daily brushing less stressful?
Took Tilly for a dental check and the vet said she needs daily brushing. Daily! I've been doing it maybe twice a week which I thought was pretty good. The vet showed me her teeth and honestly some of the back ones have a lot of tartar. She said untreated dental disease can affect the heart and kidneys which terrified me. For those of you who brush daily - does your dog actually cooperate? Tilly's pretty good but getting behind those back molars is a battle. Any tips for making it less stressful for both of us?

Daily brushing is ideal but be realistic — even 3x a week makes a huge difference vs never brushing. Don't let perfect be the enemy of good!

Enzymatic toothpaste for dogs (enzymatic means it works even without the brushing action) is a game changer if your dog tolerates brushing but not well. Poultry flavor goes down easier.

Brett C. 2026-07-11

Our beagle would bite the toothbrush bristle-first. We switched to finger cots (rubber thimble things) and he's much better now.

Karen H. 2026-07-11

Dental disease is linked to heart disease! The bacteria from gum disease enters the bloodstream and affects the heart valves. Brushing is literally heart health care.

Gary M. 2026-07-11

Greenies dental chews are great supplements to brushing but they should never REPLACE brushing. Our GSD gets one a day plus brushing 3x a week.

Rob F. 2026-07-11

Water additives (the chlorhexidine ones) are good if your dog absolutely refuses brushing. We use them as a backup on travel days.

Tracy L. 2026-07-11

Our vet said our golden's teeth were shocking at 10 despite us thinking we were doing okay. A full dental clean under anesthetic ($680) and then a strict brushing routine after — totally worth it.

Mick R. 2026-07-11

Never use human toothpaste — the fluoride and xylitol in some human toothpastes are toxic to dogs! Dog-specific only, even the 'natural' ones from the health food shop.
