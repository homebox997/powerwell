When to Start Medical Therapy for MMVD in Dogs

heart 2026-07-18

According to veterinary guidelines, dogs with stage B1 MMVD do not require medical treatment because their heart chambers are not sufficiently enlarged. Treatment is typically initiated when the disease progresses to stage B2, at which point left-sided cardiac chambers become enlarged enough to warrant therapy. Medications may include pimobendan, ACE inhibitors, and diuretics to manage volume overload and delay the onset of congestive heart failure. Regular veterinary monitoring through echocardiography and electrocardiography is essential to determine the right time to start treatment.
