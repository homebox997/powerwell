Minimally Invasive Tarsal Stabilization for Dog Leg Injuries

joint 2026-07-16

A novel minimally invasive technique has been developed for internal tarsal stabilization in dogs suffering from gastrocnemius myotendinous lesions. This procedure involves small incisions and specialized instruments to stabilize the hock joint without the need for large open surgery. The approach aims to reduce tissue trauma, decrease post-operative pain, and speed up recovery compared to traditional surgical methods. It is particularly useful for active or working dogs where preserving joint function and minimizing scarring are important. Early outcomes suggest this technique can provide stable fixation while allowing earlier return to weight-bearing activity.
