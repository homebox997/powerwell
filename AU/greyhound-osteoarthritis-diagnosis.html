Finally got my greyhound diagnosed - osteoarthritis at 10

👤 Mike T. 📅 2026-07-11 🐕 Buster, Greyhound 💬 9 comments ❤️ 238 likes 👁 2850 views
Buster the 10-year-old Greyhound diagnosed with osteoarthritis in both hind legs. On anti-inflammatories and physio. Looking for shared experiences.
Long post coming, sorry in advance. Buster's been slowing down for about 6 months. I put it down to age but my missus kept saying something wasn't right. He started limping after his morning walks, then he'd lie down halfway through and just refuse to move. Finally got him properly checked out. X-rays confirmed osteoarthritis in both hind legs. Devastating honestly. He's always been such an active dog. Vet's put him on anti-inflammatories and we've started physio. Anyone with greyhounds in similar situations? Would love to hear what's worked for your dogs. How long did it take to see improvement?

You 2026-07-11

I totally get how heartbreaking this news is, my 11-year-old greyhound went through the exact same diagnosis last winter. These lean, athletic pups carry so much strain on their delicate hind joints from their racing days, so arthritis hits them harder than most breeds. We stuck to vet-prescribed anti-inflammatories twice daily plus weekly gentle physio sessions for five weeks before seeing small wins—he stopped collapsing mid-walk and could stand up without whimpering. I also mix green-lipped mussel powder into his dinner every night to ease joint swelling. Be patient, progress creeps up slowly! Would highly recommend a non-slip ramp for your front steps too, it cuts massive pressure off their sore legs. Hope Buster finds relief soon.
