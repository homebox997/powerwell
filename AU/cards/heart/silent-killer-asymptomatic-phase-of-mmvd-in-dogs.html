Silent Killer: Asymptomatic Phase of MMVD in Dogs

heart 2026-07-20

Myxomatous mitral valve disease (MMVD) often has a long asymptomatic phase, sometimes lasting years, where the dog appears perfectly healthy despite having a heart murmur. During this stage, the heart compensates for the leaky valve by enlarging and pumping harder. Owners may notice no signs at all. This is why regular vet checkups are vital—a murmur can be detected before any symptoms occur. Without screening, the first sign of disease might be a sudden crisis like difficulty breathing from fluid in the lungs. Early detection during this silent phase allows for proactive monitoring and better long-term management.
