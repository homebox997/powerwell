Osteoarthritis Signs in Dogs: Pain, Stiffness, and Lameness

joint 2026-07-16

Dogs with osteoarthritis (OA) commonly show lameness, restricted range of motion in the affected joint, activity-related pain, and reduced overall mobility. These symptoms often worsen over time and can significantly lower a dog's quality of life. In advanced or refractory cases, dogs may not respond well to standard treatments, and signs like limping after exercise, stiffness in the morning, and reluctance to jump or climb stairs become more pronounced. The stifle (knee) and elbow joints are among the most frequently affected sites, often secondary to conditions like cranial cruciate ligament rupture or elbow dysplasia.
