Survival and Rehospitalization Rates in Dogs with CPE

heart 2026-07-18

A prospective study of 100 dogs with myxomatous mitral valve disease (MMVD) hospitalized for cardiogenic pulmonary edema (CPE) found that 95% survived to hospital discharge. However, among the 93 dogs with follow-up data, 17 (18.3%) experienced cardiac death and 22 (23.7%) required re-hospitalization for CPE within two months of discharge. These findings highlight that while short-term survival is high, the risk of serious complications remains significant in the weeks following hospitalization.
