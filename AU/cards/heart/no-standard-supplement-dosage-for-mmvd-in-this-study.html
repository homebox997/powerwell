No Standard Supplement Dosage for MMVD in This Study

heart 2026-07-18

This clinical study on dogs with myxomatous mitral valve disease (MMVD) and cardiogenic pulmonary edema did not evaluate or recommend any specific supplement dosages. The focus was on clinical parameters like respiratory rate, blood pressure, and medication (e.g., parenteral inotropes). For supplements often discussed in heart disease (e.g., taurine, omega-3 fatty acids, coenzyme Q10), pet owners should consult a veterinarian for evidence-based dosing, as inappropriate use may interfere with prescribed heart medications.

Tags: supplement caution, MMVD, heart disease, veterinary advice, no dosage data
Source: Clinical findings and risk factors for clinical outcomes in dogs with myxomatous mitral valve disease hospitalized for cardiogenic pulmonary edema - PMC
