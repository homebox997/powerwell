Dog suddenly refusing his kibble - any ideas?

👤 Gary T. 📅 2026-07-11 🐕 Bruno, Bull Arab 💬 8 comments ❤️ 140 likes 👁 2180 views
9-year-old Bull Arab suddenly refusing his usual kibble. Warming it up helps. Could indicate health issue or just senior pickiness?
Bruno's 9 and has eaten the same dry food his whole life without complaint. All of a sudden he's turning his nose up at it. Same brand, same flavour, same bowl. Just... refusing. We've tried warming it up slightly, adding a bit of hot water. He'll eat it that way but I feel like I'm giving in. Could this be a sign of something? He's drinking fine, energy seems normal. Or is this just senior dog pickiness? Has anyone else dealt with sudden food refusal?

Dave T. 2026-07-11

Refusing kibble suddenly in a senior dog should always warrant a vet visit — teeth problems are the number one cause. Our staffy refused food for 2 days before we found a broken molar.

Tracy L. 2026-07-11

Warming the kibble with a bit of hot water (not boiling) releases the aroma and makes it more palatable. Our golden started eating again immediately when we started doing this.

Karen H. 2026-07-11

Add a spoonful of wet food or bone broth to the kibble — moisture + flavor. Just make sure it doesn't replace the dry food entirely if dental health is a concern.

Brett C. 2026-07-11

Kibble freshness matters more than people think! Once opened, kibble goes stale within 6 weeks even in the bag. Buy smaller bags if your dog is a slow eater.

Rob F. 2026-07-11

Change in appetite can be medication-related too. Our border collie's Previcox killed his appetite until we started giving it with food rather than separately.

Gary M. 2026-07-11

Try hand feeding for a few days — our GSD started eating again when we sat with him and hand-fed him. Sometimes it's anxiety not taste.

Nina B. 2026-07-11

Senior dogs have reduced smell which reduces appetite. Warming food is the number one tip from our vet for old dog appetite issues. 30 seconds in microwave.

Mick R. 2026-07-11

Our greyhound went off food for a week after a medication change. We tempted him with boiled chicken breast (bland, smelly) mixed with his kibble. Got him eating again.
