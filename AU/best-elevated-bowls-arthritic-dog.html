Best elevated dog bowls for arthritic dogs?

👤 Kevin S. 📅 2026-07-11 🐕 Barney, French Bulldog 💬 8 comments ❤️ 155 likes 👁 3120 views
Vet recommended elevated bowls for Barney's joints. French Bulldog, 8 years old. Looking for stable, good-quality recommendations.
Barney's vet mentioned raised bowls might be easier on his joints when he's eating. He's 8 and has started to struggle getting down to floor level. Has anyone got recommendations for good elevated bowl stands? Looking for something stable - Barney's a bit of a clutz and I'm worried he'll knock it over. Also wondering if the height actually makes a meaningful difference or if this is more of a comfort thing? Happy to spend a bit if it'll help him.

Jen W. 2026-07-11

We have the metallic ones from Petbarn — adjustable height, non-slip base, dishwasher safe. About $55 for the set. Had them for 2 years and still going strong.

Karen H. 2026-07-11

Height matters! Rule of thumb: bowl height at your dog's elbow level when standing. Too high = neck strain, too low = neck strain. Measure your dog!

Gary M. 2026-07-11

Plastic bowls harbor bacteria even when they look clean. Stainless steel or ceramic are way more hygienic. Our GSD's skin improved when we switched bowls too.

Dave T. 2026-07-11

For really severe arthritis, consider a tilted bowl (about 15 degrees) — reduces neck extension significantly. Some dogs eat way better with a tilt.

Brett C. 2026-07-11

We bought the 'ToeTo10' elevated bowls from Amazon (about $40 delivered) and they're solid. Non-slip feet are important — our old beagle kept pushing his across the floor.

Tracy L. 2026-07-11

Our golden boy has the large elevated feeder stand from Gateway Pet. Holds two big bowls, at the perfect height, and he eats way more comfortably now than when he was hunched over the floor bowl.

Rob F. 2026-07-11

Anti-slip mats under the bowls are essential. We use the rubber shelf liner from Bunnings ($6 for a big roll) cut to size. No more bowls sliding across the floor mid-meal!

Sue P. 2026-07-11

For kelpies and working dogs, the angled front-opening design means less neck movement — they can eat in a more natural standing position. Worth paying extra for.
