Dog biscuits as training treats - okay for a 10-year-old?

👤 Maria G. 📅 2026-07-11 🐕 Poppy, Jack Russell Terrier 💬 8 comments ❤️ 176 likes 👁 3240 views
Still training 10-year-old Jack Russell with biscuits. Worried about additives and whether there are better treat options for senior dogs.
Poppy's 10 but we still do daily training with her because it keeps her mind active. We use small dog biscuits as rewards. Are these okay for a 10-year-old? I worry about the ingredients - some of them have quite a few additives. Is there a better treat option for senior dogs that still works for training? She responds really well to food rewards so I'd rather not switch methods entirely.

Dave T. 2026-07-11

Soft treats only for senior dogs! Hard crunchy treats risk dental fractures in older dogs with weaker teeth. Our vet gave us this tip after our 12yo staffy chipped a tooth on a dental chew.

Karen H. 2026-07-11

Small treats are better — senior dogs often have reduced activity so fewer calories needed. Tiny pea-sized pieces add up to a lot less than big treats.

Brett C. 2026-07-11

Freeze-dried liver treats from the pet shop are low calorie, smelly (dogs love them), and soft enough for old teeth. Our beagle gets 3-4 as a training reward.

Rob F. 2026-07-11

We use the dog's regular kibble as training treats if he'll work for it. No extra calories, familiar food, easy! Our border collie couldn't care less about kibble as a treat though.

Gary M. 2026-07-11

Blueberries are a surprisingly good low-calorie training treat for dogs! Our GSD goes crazy for them and they're full of antioxidants for senior brain health.

Tracy L. 2026-07-11

Cheese cubes (low-fat string cheese cut small) work incredibly well for high-value recalls. Our golden will come from the other end of the park for a cheese cube.

Nina B. 2026-07-11

Turkey or chicken deli slices (no seasoning!) torn into tiny pieces are our go-to for senior training. Low fat, high reward, easy to tear small.

Mick R. 2026-07-11
