Warning: Avoid High-Sodium Diets in Dogs with MMVD

heart 2026-07-18

Although this study did not directly examine diet, dogs with myxomatous mitral valve disease (MMVD) and a history of cardiogenic pulmonary edema (CPE) should avoid high-sodium foods. Excess sodium can increase blood volume and blood pressure, worsening fluid retention and straining the heart. Common high-sodium items include commercial treats, table scraps, cheese, and processed meats. A veterinarian-recommended low-sodium diet can help manage heart failure and reduce the risk of recurrent pulmonary edema episodes.
