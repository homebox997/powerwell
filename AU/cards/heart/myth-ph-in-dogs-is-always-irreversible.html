Myth: PH in Dogs Is Always Irreversible

heart 2026-07-18

A common misconception is that once a dog develops pulmonary hypertension (PH), the condition is permanent and untreatable. However, this study shows that PH secondary to myxomatous mitral valve disease (MMVD) can be reversible after mitral valve repair surgery. The study found that echocardiographic measures of pulmonary pressure significantly decreased after surgery, and 71.4% of dogs achieved complete resolution of clinical signs. While some dogs had residual or recurrent PH, the majority benefited from surgical treatment. Early intervention before irreversible pulmonary vascular remodeling occurs improves the chances of successful PH resolution.
