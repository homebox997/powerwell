Has anyone tried Librela injection for arthritis? Vets recommendation

👤 David K. 📅 2026-07-11 🐕 Oscar, Labrador 💬 8 comments ❤️ 239 likes 👁 2620 views
Vet recommended monthly Librela injection for Oscar's arthritis. Looking for owner experiences before committing - cost, side effects, interactions.
My vet recently suggested trying Librela for Oscar's arthritis. She said it's a monthly injection that targets nerve pain associated with OA. Has anyone used it? Oscar's 8 and has had hip dysplasia since he was 2. We've managed it with weight control and anti-inflammatories but his bad days are getting more frequent. Wanted to get some real owner experiences before committing. What's the cost like? Any side effects I should know about? He's already on other meds so worried about interactions. Appreciate any insights from people who've been down this path.

Librela has been incredible for our border collie. First shot took about 10 days to kick in so don't panic if you don't see results immediately. By shot 2 she was like a different dog.

Around $160-200 per injection depending on your clinic. Some vets do a package deal if you commit to 4 sessions upfront. Worth asking!

Dave T. 2026-07-11

Librela is a monoclonal antibody therapy — same principle as the Librela for humans (frunevetmab). Very safe profile from what I've read. Much easier on the liver than long-term NSAIDs.

Tracy L. 2026-07-11

Our vet was skeptical at first but she did her own research and now recommends it for all her senior OA patients. The difference is honestly remarkable.

Mick R. 2026-07-11

Downsides — it needs to be given by a vet every month (can't do at home), and it doesn't work for every dog. Maybe 80% response rate from what I've seen on the vet forums.

Jen W. 2026-07-11

Cavoodles seem to respond really well from what I've seen in the Facebook groups. Most people say it's been worth it for their quality of life.
