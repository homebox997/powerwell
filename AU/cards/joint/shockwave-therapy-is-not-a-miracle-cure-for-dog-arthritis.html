Shockwave Therapy Is Not a Miracle Cure for Dog Arthritis

joint 2026-07-16

A common misconception is that extracorporeal shockwave therapy (ESWT) can completely reverse or cure osteoarthritis in dogs. In reality, current evidence shows that ESWT produces only modest, inconsistent improvements, especially in dogs with advanced, treatment-refractory disease. It does not regenerate cartilage or eliminate the underlying joint degeneration. While it can reduce pain and improve weight-bearing in some dogs, it is best considered an adjunctive therapy—not a replacement for weight management, controlled exercise, anti-inflammatory medications, or joint supplements. Pet owners should have realistic expectations and consult their veterinarian for a comprehensive OA management plan.
