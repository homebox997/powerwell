CRP Peaks 24-48 Hours After Cardiac Damage

heart 2026-07-19

C-reactive protein (CRP) is a sensitive but non-specific acute-phase protein in dogs. Following tissue damage or inflammation, CRP levels rise within 4 hours and peak at 24-48 hours. This makes it useful for detecting recent cardiac inflammation or injury, such as in dilated cardiomyopathy or pulmonary hypertension. However, because CRP can rise from any inflammation, it must be interpreted alongside other tests. Normal CRP is typically below 10 mg/L.
