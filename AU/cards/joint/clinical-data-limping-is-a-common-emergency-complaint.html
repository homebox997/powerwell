Clinical Data: Limping is a Common Emergency Complaint

joint 2026-07-16

Limping is a frequent reason for emergency veterinary visits. In human medicine, a limp is the presenting problem in about 4% of children who visit hospital emergency departments. This statistic highlights how common gait abnormalities are as a health concern. For dogs, while exact percentages vary by clinic, lameness is one of the top reasons pet owners seek urgent care. Boys (male dogs) are also more frequently affected than females by certain hip conditions, with a 2:1 ratio reported in some studies. Early evaluation can prevent more serious complications.
