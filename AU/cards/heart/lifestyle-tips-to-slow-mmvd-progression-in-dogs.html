Lifestyle Tips to Slow MMVD Progression in Dogs

heart 2026-07-20

While myxomatous mitral valve disease (MMVD) is a progressive condition, certain lifestyle measures can help slow its advancement and improve your dog's comfort. Maintain a healthy body weight to reduce the workload on the heart. Provide moderate, consistent exercise (e.g., short walks) but avoid strenuous activity. Feed a balanced, low-sodium diet as recommended by your veterinarian. Minimize stress and keep your dog cool in hot weather, as overheating can strain the heart. Regular veterinary checkups with echocardiograms every 6–12 months are essential to monitor disease progression and adjust treatment.
