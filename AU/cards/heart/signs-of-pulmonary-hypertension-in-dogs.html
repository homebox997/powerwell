Signs of Pulmonary Hypertension in Dogs

heart 2026-07-19

Pulmonary hypertension (PH) in dogs can be subtle. Look for exercise intolerance, coughing, fainting (syncope), and labored breathing. In heartworm disease, PH may develop due to endothelial arteritis. Elevated C-reactive protein (CRP) can be an early indicator of PH, even before X-ray changes. Dogs with dilated cardiomyopathy also risk PH. Early detection through blood tests and echocardiography improves management.
