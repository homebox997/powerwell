Myth: All Hock Injuries Require Casting or Rest Only

joint 2026-07-16

A common misconception is that all hock injuries in dogs can be treated with rest or a cast alone. While mild strains may heal with conservative management, complete gastrocnemius tendon tears or avulsions often require surgical stabilization for proper healing. Casting can lead to joint stiffness, muscle atrophy, and poor tendon alignment, resulting in chronic lameness or a permanently dropped hock. The new minimally invasive surgical technique offers a middle ground—more than rest but less invasive than traditional open surgery. Proper diagnosis via ultrasound or MRI is essential to determine the right treatment for each case.
