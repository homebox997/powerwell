Oral Dietary Supplement Shows Promise for Canine OA

joint 2026-07-16

A 2025 randomized, double-blind, controlled study evaluated an oral dietary supplement containing fish oil, avocado/soybean unsaponifiables (ASU), and phytotherapeutic extracts (turmeric, Boswellia, and grape seed) in dogs with osteoarthritis. Over 56 days, dogs receiving the supplement showed significant improvements in pain scores, mobility, and quality of life compared to a placebo group. The supplement was well-tolerated with no adverse effects. This multi-ingredient approach targets inflammation, joint structure, and oxidative stress, offering a drug-free option to manage OA symptoms. Always consult your vet before starting any new supplement regimen.

Tags: osteoarthritis treatment, dietary supplement, fish oil, ASU, turmeric, Boswellia
Source: Frontiers | A randomized, double-blind, controlled study on the efficacy of an oral dietary supplement containing fish oil, ASU and phytotherapeutic extracts in canine osteoarthritis
