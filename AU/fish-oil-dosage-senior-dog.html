Fish oil supplements - how much is too much?

👤 Victor N. 📅 2026-07-11 🐕 Bruno, Rottweiler 💬 8 comments ❤️ 53 likes 👁 580 views
7-year-old Rottweiler on fish oil for joints. Conflicting dosing advice online. What dose works for your large senior dog?
Started Bruno on fish oil for his joints on the vet's recommendation. She said 1 capsule a day but I keep reading conflicting things online about omega-3 dosing for dogs. Some sites say you can give more, some say it's dangerous. Bruno's about 45kg so I know size matters. What dose do you give your senior dog? Has anyone noticed actual visible improvements - shinier coat, better movement? How long did it take?

Karen H. 2026-07-11

General rule: 75mg EPA+DHA per 10lbs of body weight for anti-inflammatory joint support. For a 30kg lab that's about 2250mg total EPA+DHA. Most pet fish oils are measured in total omega-3 not EPA+DHA specifically so check the label.

Gary M. 2026-07-11

Our GSD gets 2000mg fish oil daily (EPA+DHA from a human pharmacy is actually cheaper and cleaner than most pet products). Vet gave the all-clear at this dose.

Dave T. 2026-07-11

Salmon oil vs fish oil — salmon oil has more DHA typically which is better for brain/cognitive health in old dogs. Standard fish oil is better for pure anti-inflammatory joint support.

Brett C. 2026-07-11

Start low and go slow! We introduced fish oil too fast and our beagle had diarrhea for a week. Now he gets half the target dose for 2 weeks before building up.

Rob F. 2026-07-11

Human fish oil capsules from the pharmacy are actually fine for dogs — just way cheaper. 1000mg EPA+DHA capsules, give 1-2 per day for a medium dog. Much better value than the pet shop stuff.

Tracy L. 2026-07-11

Watch out for vitamin A toxicity with some fish liver oils — cod liver oil specifically has very high vitamin A. Use fish body oil, not liver oil.

Nina B. 2026-07-11

Our poodle gets 500mg salmon oil daily. Coat is ridiculous now — people think she's 4 not 11. The omega-3 really shows on the coat.

Mick R. 2026-07-11

Has anyone compared Nordic Naturals to generic pharmacy fish oil for dogs? The price difference is massive but are there quality differences that matter?
