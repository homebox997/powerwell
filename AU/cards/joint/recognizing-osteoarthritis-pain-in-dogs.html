Recognizing Osteoarthritis Pain in Dogs

joint 2026-07-16

Canine osteoarthritis (OA) is a chronic joint disease that causes pain, stiffness, and reduced mobility. Common signs include limping, difficulty rising from a lying position, reluctance to jump or climb stairs, decreased activity, and behavioral changes like irritability. Dogs may also show muscle atrophy around affected joints, joint swelling, or a stiff gait, especially after rest. These symptoms often worsen in cold or damp weather. Early recognition is key to managing OA effectively, as dogs tend to hide pain until it becomes severe. If your dog shows any of these signs, consult a veterinarian for a proper diagnosis and treatment plan.
