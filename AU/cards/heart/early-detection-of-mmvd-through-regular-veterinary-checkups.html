Early Detection of MMVD Through Regular Veterinary Checkups

heart 2026-07-18

Since MMVD progresses slowly with age and often shows no early symptoms, routine veterinary screenings are crucial for early detection. The American College of Veterinary Internal Medicine categorizes MMVD into four stages, with stage B being preclinical. Regular auscultation for heart murmurs, followed by echocardiography and electrocardiography, can identify dogs in stages B1 and B2 before heart failure develops. Early detection allows for timely intervention, especially in high-risk breeds like Cavalier King Charles Spaniels, where the disease may progress faster than in other breeds.
