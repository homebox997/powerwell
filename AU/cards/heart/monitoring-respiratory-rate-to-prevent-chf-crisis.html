Monitoring Respiratory Rate to Prevent CHF Crisis

heart 2026-07-18

For dogs with myxomatous mitral valve disease (MMVD), regular monitoring of resting respiratory rate at home can help detect early signs of congestive heart failure (CHF) before a crisis. A normal resting rate is typically below 30–40 breaths per minute. A sustained increase, especially above 40–50 breaths per minute, may signal fluid accumulation in the lungs (pulmonary edema). Early detection allows for prompt veterinary intervention, potentially reducing the need for emergency hospitalization and improving outcomes.
