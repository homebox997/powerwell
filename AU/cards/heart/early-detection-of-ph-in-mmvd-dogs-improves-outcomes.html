Early Detection of PH in MMVD Dogs Improves Outcomes

heart 2026-07-18

Early detection of pulmonary hypertension (PH) in dogs with myxomatous mitral valve disease (MMVD) is crucial for better treatment outcomes. According to ACVIM consensus guidelines, routine echocardiographic screening can identify PH before severe symptoms develop. This study found that mitral valve repair was effective in reducing pulmonary pressure and resolving clinical signs in most dogs. Regular cardiac check-ups, including Doppler echocardiography to measure tricuspid regurgitation velocity and left atrial size, allow veterinarians to detect worsening heart disease early and consider surgical intervention before irreversible pulmonary vascular changes occur.
