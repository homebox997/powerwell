Prevalence of MMVD in Older Dogs: 30-70%

heart 2026-07-18

Myxomatous mitral valve disease affects between 30% and 70% of the canine population over 10 years of age, making it the most prevalent heart disease in dogs. This wide range reflects differences in breed susceptibility, diagnostic criteria, and study populations. Small breed dogs, particularly Cavalier King Charles Spaniels, Dachshunds, and Miniature Poodles, are at highest risk. The disease progresses slowly, and many dogs remain in preclinical stages (B1 or B2) for years without developing heart failure. Regular cardiac screening is recommended for all senior small-breed dogs.
