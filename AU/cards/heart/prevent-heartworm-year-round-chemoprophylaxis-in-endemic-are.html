Prevent Heartworm: Year-Round Chemoprophylaxis in Endemic Areas

heart 2026-07-21

Heartworm disease, caused by the parasite Dirofilaria immitis, is transmitted by mosquitoes and is widespread in many regions. The best prevention is year-round chemoprophylaxis using medications prescribed by a veterinarian. In hyperendemic areas, like the Canary Islands where this study was conducted, consistent prevention is vital. Dogs that had never received heartworm prevention were included in this study, highlighting the risk of infection. Owners should discuss appropriate preventives (oral, topical, or injectable) with their vet, especially in warm climates where mosquitoes are active year-round.
