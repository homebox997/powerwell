Ramp for car - is it worth getting one? Mine hates the boot

👤 Paul R. 📅 2026-07-11 🐕 Ginger, Maltese 💬 8 comments ❤️ 203 likes 👁 2230 views
12-year-old Maltese struggling with car boot. Considering a ramp but worried dog won't use it. Successful ramp training experiences?
Ginger's 12 and getting into the car boot is getting harder for her. She's a small dog so I've always just lifted her but my back isn't what it used to be either. Looking at getting a ramp but not sure if she'll actually use it. She's set in her ways. Has anyone successfully transitioned their old dog to a ramp? What brand did you get? Also worried she might try to jump anyway and hurt herself. How long did it take for your dog to trust the ramp?

8 Comments

Jen W. 2026-07-11

We have the PetSafe Happy Ride folding ramp — folds flat, fits in the boot, aluminium so not too heavy. Our cavoodle uses it independently now which was the whole goal!

Karen H. 2026-07-11

The 3-step vs ramp debate is real. For a small dog with bad hips, a ramp is way better than steps because it reduces the vertical climb. Steps are fine for early-stage mobility issues.

Gary M. 2026-07-11

Ramp angle matters — if it's too steep the dog won't use it. PetSafe ramps have adjustable angles. Aim for under 20 degrees if your dog has significant OA.

Dave T. 2026-07-11

Non-slip surface on the ramp is non-negotiable. Ours had a smooth aluminium surface originally and Molly refused it until we stuck anti-slip grip tape along it.

Brett C. 2026-07-11

We trained our beagle with treats on the ramp for 2 weeks before trusting him on it unsupervised. Positive association with the ramp is everything.

Tracy L. 2026-07-11

For golden retrievers and larger dogs, the ramps are heavy! Look for ones with a carry handle and consider whether you can actually lift it in and out of the car yourself.

Rob F. 2026-07-11

Truck/SUV owners: measure your tailgate height carefully. Some ramps are too short for 4WDs. We had to return two before finding the right length.

Nina B. 2026-07-11

There's a facebook group 'Dog Ramps and Stairs Australia' — heaps of real owner reviews of different brands. Way more helpful than Amazon reviews.
