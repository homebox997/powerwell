Myth: A Limp Without Pain is Not Serious

joint 2026-07-16

A common misconception is that a non-painful limp is harmless or doesn't require a vet visit. This is false. A non-painful limp can be caused by serious mechanical issues like hip dysplasia, leg length differences, or even early-stage bone cancer (osteosarcoma). In children, conditions like Legg-Calvé-Perthes disease cause a painless limp that leads to bone deformity if untreated. In dogs, a painless limp should still be investigated, especially if it persists. Always consult a vet to rule out underlying structural or degenerative problems.
