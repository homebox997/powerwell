Prevalence of MMVD: A Common Heart Disease in Small Dogs

heart 2026-07-20

Myxomatous mitral valve disease (MMVD) is the most common acquired heart disease in dogs, accounting for approximately 75% of all canine heart disease cases. It is especially prevalent in small-breed dogs, with nearly 100% of Cavalier King Charles Spaniels developing the condition by age 10. Other predisposed breeds include Dachshunds, Miniature Poodles, and Cocker Spaniels. The disease is degenerative, meaning it worsens over time, and is the leading cause of congestive heart failure in dogs. These statistics underscore the importance of routine cardiac screening in at-risk breeds.
