Treating Transient Synovitis: Rest and Symptom Care

joint 2026-07-16

Transient synovitis is a reactive arthritis of the hip joint, often of unknown cause, and is a common cause of limping in young animals. Dogs with this condition are usually able to walk and may have a low-grade fever, but they do not appear severely ill. Treatment is primarily supportive: rest, anti-inflammatory medication, and pain relief as prescribed by a vet. The condition typically resolves on its own within one week. However, diagnosis is made only after excluding more serious causes like infection or fracture. Never assume a limp is 'just synovitis' without a vet exam.
