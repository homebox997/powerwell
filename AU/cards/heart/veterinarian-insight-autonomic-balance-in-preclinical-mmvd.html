Veterinarian Insight: Autonomic Balance in Preclinical MMVD

heart 2026-07-18

Veterinary cardiologists emphasize that dogs with preclinical MMVD (stages B1 and B2) often maintain adequate autonomic balance. A study by Santos et al. (2024) showed that despite cardiac remodeling in stage B2, the sympathovagal balance—the interplay between sympathetic and parasympathetic nervous systems—remains stable. This indicates that the body compensates well in these early stages. Experts recommend monitoring HRV as a non-invasive tool to assess autonomic function, but routine HRV changes alone should not prompt immediate treatment changes without considering other clinical and echocardiographic findings.
