Hey everyone. Bit of a downer post but I need some advice. Charlie is 9 now and I've started noticing him really struggling on the stairs. He used to bound up no worries, but these days he hesitates at the top step like he's psyching himself up. Sometimes I catch him whimpering when he thinks I'm not looking. Took him to the vet last month and she mentioned something about arthritis starting. She didn't seem too concerned but I am. Has anyone else been through this? What did you do? Are supplements worth it? I've seen so many different brands at the pet shop and online.

Sarah, my heart absolutely broke reading this—this exact stair hesitation was the first warning sign I spotted with my 10-year-old Golden Retriever, Max, two years ago, so I know exactly that sinking, worried feeling you’re carrying right now. Our vet also flagged mild early arthritis at first and brushed it off as normal ageing, but that quiet whimpering when climbing stairs told me his discomfort was far more noticeable than they made it sound, and I wish I’d acted faster to ease his daily pain sooner. I tested dozens of joint supplements over six months before landing on two consistent game-changers: pure green-lipped mussel powder daily mixed into his dinner, plus low-dose fish oil capsules to calm joint inflammation. The difference after four weeks was night and day—he stopped freezing mid-stair and rarely whimpered overnight. I also grabbed a wide, non-slip foam ramp from Bunnings for our front steps; it cost under $50 and eliminated nearly all of his painful uphill strain entirely. Slow, gentle hydrotherapy swimming sessions at our local vet clinic helped build supporting muscle without harsh pressure on his hips too, one session a week worked wonders. A quick note to ease your stress: early arthritis caught this early is incredibly manageable with small daily adjustments, you don’t need expensive injections straight away. I’d highly recommend asking your vet for a mild anti-inflammatory maintenance dose for bad weather days—cold mornings always flare Max’s joint stiffness the worst. It’s such a tough shift watching our once energetic seniors slow down, but your care and attention already mean Charlie’s pain will be so much lighter. Hope these tricks help you both find more easy, pain-free days together soon!
