Extracorporeal Shockwave Therapy for Refractory Canine OA

joint 2026-07-16

Extracorporeal shockwave therapy (ESWT) is a non-invasive treatment used as an adjunct for canine osteoarthritis, especially in dogs that do not respond well to conventional therapies. In a recent double-blinded trial, dogs with advanced elbow or stifle OA received one or two sessions of focused ESWT (1000 pulses at 0.14 mJ/mm²) about 28 days apart. The treatment was well tolerated with no major adverse events. However, improvements were modest and inconsistent, with slightly better results seen in dogs that received two sessions. ESWT may offer some benefit but is not a cure for end-stage OA.

Tags: ESWT, shockwave therapy, non-invasive treatment, refractory osteoarthritis, canine joint therapy
Source: Effects of Extracorporeal Shockwave Therapy on Pain and Mobility in Client-Owned Dogs with Refractory Elbow and Stifle Osteoarthritis: A Randomized Double-Blinded Trial - PMC
