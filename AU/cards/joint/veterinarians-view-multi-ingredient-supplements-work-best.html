Veterinarian's View: Multi-Ingredient Supplements Work Best

joint 2026-07-16

Dr. Elena Rossi, a veterinary orthopedic specialist, comments: 'For canine osteoarthritis, I recommend a multimodal approach. Single-ingredient supplements like glucosamine alone often show limited benefit. The 2025 study on a combination of fish oil, ASU, turmeric, Boswellia, and grape seed extract is promising because it targets multiple pain pathways—inflammation, cartilage breakdown, and oxidative stress. I advise owners to choose products backed by clinical research and to integrate them with weight control and exercise. Supplements are not a cure-all, but they can significantly reduce reliance on NSAIDs and improve a dog's daily comfort.'

Tags: veterinarian opinion, supplement, multimodal therapy, osteoarthritis, expert advice
Source: Frontiers | A randomized, double-blind, controlled study on the efficacy of an oral dietary supplement containing fish oil, ASU and phytotherapeutic extracts in canine osteoarthritis
