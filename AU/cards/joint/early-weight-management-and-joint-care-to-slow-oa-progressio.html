Early Weight Management and Joint Care to Slow OA Progression

joint 2026-07-16

Preventing or slowing osteoarthritis in dogs starts early with weight management, controlled exercise, and joint-friendly nutrition. Keeping your dog at a healthy body condition score (BCS) reduces excess stress on joints, particularly the elbows and stifles. Regular, low-impact activities like swimming or leash walks help maintain muscle strength and joint flexibility without causing further damage. For breeds prone to elbow dysplasia or cruciate ligament issues, early screening and preventive care are critical. Once OA develops, it is progressive, so early intervention with joint supplements and veterinary guidance can delay severe symptoms.
