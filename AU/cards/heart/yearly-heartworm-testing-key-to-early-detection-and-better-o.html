Yearly Heartworm Testing: Key to Early Detection and Better Outcomes

heart 2026-07-21

Heartworm disease can remain silent for months or even years, causing progressive damage to the pulmonary arteries before symptoms appear. Regular annual testing (antigen and microfilaria tests) is essential for early detection, especially in endemic areas. Early diagnosis allows veterinarians to assess for complications like pulmonary hypertension using tools such as NT-proBNP measurement and echocardiography before initiating treatment. Dogs diagnosed early have a much better prognosis and lower risk of severe vascular damage. Prevention with monthly heartworm preventives remains the gold standard, but consistent testing ensures any breakthrough infections are caught promptly.
