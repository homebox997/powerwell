Warning: Never Give Human Painkillers to a Limping Dog

joint 2026-07-16

If your dog is limping, never give them human pain relievers like ibuprofen, acetaminophen (Tylenol), or naproxen (Aleve). These drugs are highly toxic to dogs and can cause severe gastrointestinal ulcers, kidney failure, liver damage, or even death. Even aspirin, though sometimes used by vets, must be dosed carefully under professional guidance. The safest approach is to restrict your dog's activity and see a veterinarian for proper diagnosis and pain management. Veterinary-specific NSAIDs (e.g., carprofen, meloxicam) are safer and more effective for canine pain.
