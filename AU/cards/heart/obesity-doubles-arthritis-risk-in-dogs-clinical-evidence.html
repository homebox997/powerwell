Obesity Doubles Arthritis Risk in Dogs: Clinical Evidence

heart 2026-07-18

Clinical studies show that overweight dogs are significantly more likely to develop osteoarthritis. Research indicates that dogs at an ideal body weight have a much lower incidence of hip and elbow dysplasia compared to obese dogs. One landmark study found that a lean feeding regimen reduced the prevalence and severity of hip osteoarthritis in Labrador Retrievers by nearly 50% over their lifetime. Excess body fat not only increases mechanical load on joints but also produces inflammatory substances that worsen joint degeneration.
