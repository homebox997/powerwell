Vessel Narrowing and Degeneration in Dog Atria

heart 2026-07-21

In a comparative study of 31 dogs, those with chronic mitral valve disease (CMVD) showed more distinct vessel narrowing in the left atrial wall than dogs with dilated cardiomyopathy (DCM) (median lumen area ratio 0.26 vs. 0.3; p < 0.05). Conversely, dogs with DCM exhibited more severe degenerative changes in cardiomyocytes, including greater nuclei enlargement (score 3 vs. 1), loss of striation (score 3 vs. 2), and structural alterations (score 3 vs. 2; all p < 0.05). These distinct pathological patterns suggest different mechanisms of atrial damage in the two diseases.
