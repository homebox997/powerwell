Homemade bone broth - is it actually good for old dogs?

👤 Sandra L. 📅 2026-07-11 🐕 Maggie, Australian Cattle Dog 💬 8 comments ❤️ 195 likes 👁 1780 views
Mum makes bone broth for 11-year-old Cattle Dog. Claimed benefits for joints. Seeking evidence on whether bone broth actually helps senior dogs.
My mum's been making bone broth for Maggie and says it's brilliant for old dogs' joints. She's been giving Maggie a spoonful with her dinner for years. Is there actual evidence behind this or is it one of those grandma remedies? I don't want to dismiss it if it's genuinely helpful but also don't want to give Maggie something unnecessary. Anyone know if bone broth actually helps with joint health in senior dogs?

8 Comments

Dave T. 2026-07-11

Homemade bone broth is SO easy. Beef marrow bones from the butcher (like $8), slow cook in crockpot for 24hrs, strain, skim the fat, freeze in ice cube trays. Our staffy's joints improved in 3 weeks.

Tracy L. 2026-07-11

The gelatin in bone broth is what supports joint health — not the cartilage (which has glucosamine but broth doesn't contain much). So technically it's more about overall gut health than direct joint support.

Karen H. 2026-07-11

Add a teaspoon of apple cider vinegar to the pot when making bone broth — it helps extract more minerals from the bones including glucosamine and chondroitin precursors.

Brett C. 2026-07-11

Be careful with sodium! Dogs with heart issues should have LOW sodium broth. For senior dogs generally, make it with LOW salt or no salt added.

Rob F. 2026-07-11

We freeze bone broth in ice cube trays and give 2 cubes per day mixed with food. Easy, cheap, and our beagle's coat is noticeably shinier too.

Gary M. 2026-07-11

My vet actually recommended bone broth as a 'gateway' to get our GSD to accept his joint supplements (we hide the powder in it). Bonus!

Nina B. 2026-07-11

How long does bone broth last in the fridge? We make a big batch and want to make sure it doesn't go off before we use it all.

Dave T. 2026-07-11

5 days in the fridge, 6 months in the freezer. Always smell it before giving to your dog — if it smells off at all, bin it.
