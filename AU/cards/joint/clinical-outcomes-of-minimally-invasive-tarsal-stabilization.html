Clinical Outcomes of Minimally Invasive Tarsal Stabilization

joint 2026-07-16

Clinical studies on the novel minimally invasive tarsal stabilization technique show promising results. In a case series of dogs with gastrocnemius myotendinous lesions, the majority achieved good to excellent functional recovery within 8-12 weeks post-surgery. Complications were low, with less than 10% experiencing minor issues like temporary swelling or mild infection. Radiographic follow-up confirmed stable joint alignment in over 90% of cases. Compared to historical data on open surgery, the minimally invasive approach showed faster return to weight-bearing and fewer wound complications, though long-term outcomes were similar.
